## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. arxiv:2607.16850 — Group Entropy-Controlled Policy Optimization (GEPO)
**Authors:** Guangran Cheng et al. | **Submitted:** July 18, 2026

**Core claim:** GRPO-style z-score normalization creates an entropy-dependent bias across
prompt groups in heterogeneous task mixtures. Low-entropy groups (easy tasks, all rollouts
succeed) produce inflated advantage magnitudes relative to high-entropy groups (hard tasks,
diverse outcomes), causing easy-task gradients to crowd out hard-task learning.

**Method:** Entropy-conditioned asymmetric advantage attenuation at the group level:
- Low-entropy groups: attenuate positive advantages by `(H_g / H_low)` where `H_low = P25(H_g)`
- High-entropy groups: attenuate negative advantages by `(H_high / H_g)` where `H_high = P75(H_g)`
- Thresholds are percentile-based (self-calibrating, no tuning)

**Relevance to our pipeline:** Directly targets Root Problem A (ACR=52.4%) from a new angle —
entropy-conditioned group discrimination rather than variance injection (AVSPO) or
batch-level rebalancing (FADE). Our HumanEval task mix is maximally heterogeneous (62/82
easy, 20/82 hard), making it an ideal test case.

### 2. arxiv:2606.21090 — Self-Improvement Can Self-Regress: The Rise-and-Collapse Failure Mode
**Submitted:** June 2026

**Core claim:** In REINFORCE/GRPO post-training for code generation, pass@1 peaks within
tens of gradient steps and then falls back to near-zero within the SAME training run.
KL constraints and EWC do NOT prevent it. Mechanism: probability mass concentrates on
top-1 rollout → diversity drops → GRPO advantage variance drops → ACR triggers →
self-reinforcing collapse.

**Relevance:** Our full arm stagnates at 92.68% = router arm across 4 cycles. The standard
explanation is ACR=52.4%. But if the intra-cycle peak occurs at step 10–20 and we save
the step-100 checkpoint, we always load a collapsed model. This is testable with per-epoch
eval logging (EXP-127). If confirmed, the fix is trivial: save best-epoch checkpoint.
