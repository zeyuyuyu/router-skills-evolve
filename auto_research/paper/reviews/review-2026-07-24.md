### W5 — No formal theorem or proposition for Zero-Variance result (WRITING, CARRYOVER)
**Location:** Section 7 / Zero-Variance analysis  
The Group-SD Identity (Bay & Yearick, 2607.00152) provides a formal algebraic proof that σ=0 → zero gradient. The paper cites this but does not formalize our 52.4% finding as a Proposition or Corollary. AAAI reviewers reward rigorous claim formatting in systems papers.  
**Suggested text (implementable this week):**  
```
Proposition 1 (Zero-Gradient Groups). Under DAPO with dynamic sampling (drop groups if σ=0),
let K denote the rollout count per group. For a group where all K outcomes are identical
(all-pass or all-fail), the within-group reward variance σ² = 0. By the Group-SD Identity
[groupsd2026], the DAPO advantage A_i = (r_i - μ)/σ is undefined (0/0), clipped to 0.
Consequently, the policy gradient for that group is exactly zero. In our 4-cycle
HumanEval experiments, 46.3%–52.4% of training groups per cycle satisfy this condition.
```
**Fix:** Add Proposition 1 box to §5.4 — no A800 needed.
