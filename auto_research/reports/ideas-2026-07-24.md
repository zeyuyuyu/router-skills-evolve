## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. arxiv:2607.04364 — RL Forgets! Towards Continual Policy Optimization
**July 4, 2026**

Standard RL post-training assumes the policy does not catastrophically forget previously acquired capabilities during training. This assumption breaks in multi-cycle continual post-training. The paper proposes CPO (Continual Policy Optimization), a replay-free framework combining:
- **Prior-task behavioral KL regularization**: KL(π_θ || π_SFT) over the current rollout batch
- **Parameter-movement regularization**: L_reg = λ × ||θ − θ_SFT||² (L2-SP from SFT checkpoint)

Key results on Qwen3-VL-8B: -13.7% forgetting, +7.0% pretrained capability retained on MRCL benchmark vs standard RL.

**Why it matters for us (Hypothesis D):** Our Full arm = Router arm across all 4 cycles. Beyond the ACR issue (zero-variance groups), there is an unexplored hypothesis: GRPO may be catastrophically forgetting Phase 3a SFT gains during Phase 3b training. GRPO gradients for hard-task groups (advantage ≠ 0) may push model weights in directions that inadvertently degrade easy-task performance (where advantage = 0 because all rollouts pass). The resulting GRPO adapter quality ≤ SFT quality → Full = Router. CPO-PMP directly tests this hypothesis. No prior experiment in the queue tests Hypothesis D.

### 2. arxiv:2606.18810 — Learning from Own Solutions: Self-Conditioned Credit Assignment for RL with Verifiable Rewards
**Shan et al., June 2026**

Standard GRPO assigns uniform advantage across all tokens of a rollout, ignoring which specific tokens were responsible for success vs. failure. For tasks with verifiable rewards (code: pass/fail), the model's own successful solutions provide exact credit anchors: the first token position where a failed rollout diverges from the nearest successful rollout is where the model made a wrong policy decision.

SCCA assigns credit based on token distance to this divergence point:
- `A_token(f, t) = A_group × max(min_weight, 1 − decay × |t − div_pos| / seq_len)`
- Successful rollouts: standard credit (weight = 1)
- ACR groups (all-pass/all-fail): fall back to standard GRPO

Results: +3.1 pass@1 HumanEval, +2.7 pass@1 MBPP on a 7B code model, stable training.

**Why it matters for us:** Our non-ACR groups (47.6% of 82 HumanEval groups) carry ALL GRPO signal. Better credit concentration in these groups → faster gradient signal → GRPO adapter improves faster. HumanEval has syntactically clear divergence points (wrong algorithm, off-by-one, wrong return) making token-level divergence reliable.

### 3. arxiv:2607.01763 — Denser ≠ Better: Limits of On-Policy Self-Distillation for Continual Post-Training
**Wang et al., July 2, 2026**

Shows that on-policy self-distillation (SDPO) accelerates in-domain specialization but causes stronger forgetting and can collapse in continual post-training, while GRPO adapts more conservatively. Denser self-distillation induces larger drift in parameter and response space, and amplifies formatting artifacts via a self-reinforcing teacher-student loop.

**Relevance to our skills arm:** Our skills distillation uses on-policy exemplars (SKILL_MAX_EXEMPLARS=50, SKILL_DISTILL_N=50). If the "denser ≠ better" finding applies, increasing exemplar count beyond a threshold could hurt via self-reinforcing loop. The current 75.61% skills gap may partly reflect this — though EXP-128 (SC-SDPO pass-rate weighting) directly addresses exemplar quality vs. quantity. This paper is more diagnostic than actionable given the queue backlog; included here as context for understanding the skills arm failure.

**What changes (~15 lines in grpo_train_simple.py):**

### EXP-131: SCCA — Self-Conditioned Credit Assignment for GRPO
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_24_002_scca_self_conditioned_credit_grpo_humaneval`  
**Priority:** 7 · **Kind:** `grpo_continual` · **GPU hours:** ~3.0h

**Addresses:** Problem B — 47.6% of non-ACR GRPO groups carry ALL signal; improve credit quality  
**Arxiv:** 2606.18810 (June 2026)

**What changes (~25 lines in grpo_train_simple.py):**
1. For each non-ACR group: identify successful rollouts
2. For each failed rollout, find first token divergence from nearest successful rollout
3. Apply decay-weighted credit: `weight(f, t) = max(0.1, 1 − 2.0 × |t − div_pos| / seq_len)`
4. Multiply GRPO token-level loss by SCCA weights; ACR groups fall back to standard GRPO
5. Log divergence position distribution and token-weight distribution for analysis

**Env vars:** `SCCA_ENABLED=1`, `SCCA_DECAY_RATE=2.0`, `SCCA_MIN_WEIGHT=0.1`

**Expected outcome:** Full arm 92.68% → ≥94%; non-ACR group gradient magnitude increased; faster GRPO convergence per cycle.
