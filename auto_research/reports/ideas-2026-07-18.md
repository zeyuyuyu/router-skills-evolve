## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. arxiv:2605.21125 — Advantage Collapse in Group Relative Policy Optimization: Diagnosis and Mitigation
**Xixiang He, Qiyao Sun, Ao Cheng et al., May 30, 2026 — ICML 2026**

**Core finding:** Introduces the Advantage Collapse Rate (ACR) — the fraction of
GRPO training batches where within-group reward variance is zero — as the
primary diagnostic metric for training stagnation. Shows that early-stage ACR
explains **62% of variance** in final pass@1, making it a strong predictor of
outcome before accuracy degradation is visible.

**AVSPO method:** For each collapsed group (std=0), inject m synthetic virtual
samples with the opposite reward to restore intra-group diversity. Injection
count m is adaptive: driven by real-time ACR level, m scales up when ACR exceeds
a target threshold and turns off when ACR normalizes. Key distinction: the virtual
samples affect only the advantage computation (group statistics) — they are NOT
added to the model's token-level loss.

**Key results:**
- ACR reduction: **58–63%** relative to standard GRPO across 0.5B–14B model scales
- Pass@1 gains: **+4–6pp** consistently on math/code benchmarks
- Outperforms DAPO, Dr.GRPO, and sign-advantage in the paper's ablation
- Accepted ICML 2026; code released at https://github.com/QingyongHu/AVSPO

**Relevance:** Our baseline ACR=52.4% is in the "severe collapse" regime. AVSPO is
philosophically opposite to Dr.GRPO (EXP-120) — rather than zeroing the gradient
for collapsed groups, it injects synthetic diversity to CREATE signal. Orthogonal
to RAPER (EXP-118), MDP-GRPO (EXP-119), and all ZPD-SFT work (EXP-121).

### 2. arxiv:2507.21848 — EDGE-GRPO: Entropy-Driven GRPO with Guided Error Correction for Advantage Diversity
**Xingjian Zhang, Siwei Wen, Wenjun Wu, Lei Huang — July 2025**

**Core observation:** The relationship between per-sample policy entropy H(π|x,y)
and correctness is non-monotone in GRPO:

| Sample type | Entropy | Meaning | Standard GRPO treatment | EDGE-GRPO treatment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Correct, low-H | Confident-correct | Model already knows it | Equal weight | Smaller update |
| Correct, high-H | Uncertain-correct | Valuable teacher signal | Equal weight | Larger update |
| Incorrect, low-H | Confident-wrong | Locked in bad solution | Equal weight | Larger penalty |
| Incorrect, high-H | Uncertain-wrong | Actively exploring | Equal weight | Smaller penalty |

**Two components:**
1. **Entropy-Driven Advantage (EDA):** Scale each sample's advantage by
   `tanh(α × H(π_i))` — concentrates updates on the most informative samples,
   increases effective advantage diversity within non-collapsed groups.
2. **Guided Error Correction (GEC):** For all-fail groups, inject the teacher's
   verified solution (real, not synthetic) as a reward=1 sample, breaking the
   all-fail deadlock with a high-quality reference.

**Relevance for our pipeline:** Teacher traces from `collect_traces.py`
(via `SCALING_FORCE_BOTH=1`) already provide one large-model verified solution
per task — GEC is nearly free to implement (~10 lines). EDA requires only the
logits already materialized in the GRPO forward pass (~15 lines). This is the
only algorithm in our queue that addresses BOTH Root Problem A (collapsed groups)
and Root Problem B (non-collapsed group efficiency) simultaneously.

### 3. arxiv:2605.21125 companion — GDPO: Group reward-Decoupled Normalization Policy Optimization (ICML 2026)
**ICML 2026 poster (2606 series)**

**Core finding:** Decouples reward normalization from advantage computation by
maintaining separate baseline statistics per reward-level (correct vs. incorrect
subgroups), rather than normalizing across the full mixed group. For groups with
both correct and incorrect rollouts, this prevents the correct-sample mean from
being biased by the incorrect-sample rewards and vice versa.

**Relevance:** Partially overlaps with our MDP-GRPO (EXP-119) which uses an EMA
anchor; GDPO's decoupling is a cleaner formulation. Noted for future consideration
but NOT queued today (would be EXP-124+ when cap lifts).

### EXP-122: AVSPO — Adaptive Virtual Sample Injection for Collapsed GRPO Groups
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_18_001_avspo_virtual_sample_injection_humaneval`  
**Priority:** 9 · **Kind:** `grpo_continual` · **GPU hours:** ~2.5h

**Addresses:** Root Problem (A) — ACR=52.4% / Full arm = Router arm  
**Arxiv:** 2605.21125 (ICML 2026)

**What changes:**
- Detect collapsed groups (within-group reward std < eps) after each GRPO rollout
- Compute adaptive injection count: `m = ceil(acr_current / 0.20 * 2)` where 2 = m_max
- For all-fail collapsed groups: inject m virtual reward=1 samples
- For all-pass collapsed groups: inject m virtual reward=0 samples
- Recompute group statistics (mean, std) over the augmented K+m samples
- Apply standard GRPO normalization to the augmented statistics
- Virtual samples affect advantage computation ONLY — excluded from token-level loss
- Add env vars: `AVSPO_ENABLED`, `AVSPO_ACR_TARGET=0.20`, `AVSPO_M_MAX=2`
- Total: ~20 lines in `grpo_train_simple.py`

**Why now:** AVSPO is an ICML 2026 paper directly addressing our exact metric (ACR).
EXP-120 (Dr.GRPO, queued yesterday) takes the opposite approach — zero gradient for
collapsed groups. Both approaches deserve to be tested. AVSPO's +4-6pp track record
across model scales in the paper's ablation is stronger than any single prior
experiment in our queue. Priority 9 (highest tier) reflects this.

**Expected outcome:** ACR < 22% (58% reduction from 52.4%); full arm pass@1 ≥ 93%
(+0.3pp minimum); full arm may decouple from router arm for first time since EXP-001.

### EXP-123: EDGE-GRPO — Entropy-Driven Advantage + Guided Error Correction
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_18_002_edge_grpo_entropy_driven_adv_gec_humaneval`  
**Priority:** 8 · **Kind:** `grpo_continual` · **GPU hours:** ~3.0h

**Addresses:** Root Problems (A) + (B) simultaneously — ACR and non-collapsed efficiency  
**Arxiv:** 2507.21848

**What changes:**
- **GEC (collapsed groups — Problem A):**
  Load teacher traces from `traces.jsonl` at GRPO training start (once).
  For each all-fail group, look up teacher trace by task_id; if found (reward=1,
  verified by HumanEval test suite), inject it into the group as a real sample.
  Unlike AVSPO virtual samples, GEC samples ARE included in token-level loss.
- **EDA (non-collapsed groups — Problem B):**
  Compute per-sequence entropy `H_i = mean(-sum_v p_v log p_v)` from logits
  already materialized in the GRPO forward pass.
  Scale: `A_edge(i) = A_grpo(i) * tanh(alpha * H_i)` (alpha=1.0 default).
- Add env vars: `EDGE_GRPO_ENABLED`, `EDGE_GEC_ENABLED`, `EDGE_EDA_ENABLED`,
  `EDGE_EDA_ALPHA=1.0`
- Analysis: include GEC-only vs EDA-only vs combined ablation (3 sub-runs if time)
- Total: ~30 lines in `grpo_train_simple.py` + index-build from `traces.jsonl`

**Why now:** Among all ACR-collapse fixes in the queue (EXP-108/118/119/120/122),
EDGE-GRPO is the only one that simultaneously addresses Problem B. Our pipeline
already has teacher traces per task (SCALING_FORCE_BOTH=1), so GEC is nearly free
to implement. The entropy computation reuses already-materialized logits — no extra
inference. Together, EXP-122 (AVSPO) and EXP-123 (EDGE-GRPO) give us both sides
of the injection vs. reweighting debate.

**Expected outcome:** ACR < 35% (via GEC reducing collapsed groups); full arm
pass@1 ≥ 93.5% (EDA + GEC combined, targeting both A and B). If confirmed,
this becomes the most capable GRPO variant in our ablation suite.
