## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. `arxiv:2605.00433` — RECRL: Requirement-Aware Curriculum Reinforcement Learning
*Yin, Tian, Chen, Guo — May 2026*

Automatically measures per-task difficulty as the base model's solve rate, then applies
adaptive curriculum sampling (easy→hard) during GRPO training. On 5 benchmarks across
5 LLMs: **Qwen2.5-Coder-3B improves from 60.07 → 66.50 pass@1**, enabling it to
slightly outperform Qwen2.5-Coder-7B (66.05). Average improvement over flat-GRPO
baseline: **+1.23% to +5.62%**.

**Why relevant:** Our 3B GRPO degraded (62→60). RECRL's curriculum might fix that.
Our 1.5B best case (+2pts flat) is a strong candidate for curriculum amplification.

### 2. `arxiv:2605.02944` — Exploring Pass-Rate Reward in RL for Code Generation
*May 2026*

Controlled comparison of binary vs pass-rate rewards in GRPO and RLOO. Finding:
**pass-rate rewards do not reliably outperform binary rewards** despite being denser.
The miscalibration between partial test pass rate and full-correctness gradient makes
the optimization signal weaker on hard problems.

**Why relevant:** Validates our current binary reward choice. Argues against the
handoff doc suggestion to add "partial test pass count" — or at least says it needs
careful design to avoid the miscalibration trap.

### 3. `arxiv:2503.06639` — GRPO's Effective Loss, Dynamics, and Success Amplification
*Guo et al., March 2025 (updated 2026)*

Theoretical analysis of GRPO showing: (1) the KL penalty term is equivalent to
reverse-KL from new policy to reference; (2) removing KL (as DAPO/Dr.GRPO do) helps
reasoning tasks but may cause distribution collapse on smaller models; (3) the
"success amplification" dynamic explains why GRPO degrades on problems where the base
already solves most rollouts (reward variance → 0 → no gradient).

**Why relevant:** Explains why 400-task GRPO degraded the 1.5B model (too many
easy tasks at the end → zero gradient → noise updates). Supports adding KL penalty
and/or curriculum ordering as fixes.

useful gradients; easy tasks seen late don't collapse training. Expected: 47→52+/100.

### EXP-002 · `exp_2026_05_15_002_kl_grpo_15b_300tasks` · Priority 9
**Kind:** `grpo_continual`
**Model:** Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B-Instruct
**Spec:** 300 MBPP tasks, kl_coeff=0.02 against frozen reference model,
4 rollouts/prompt, 1 epoch, lr=5e-6, LoRA r=16, qwen-chat, binary reward, eval@100

**Rationale:** 200-task GRPO gives +2pts; 400-task degrades -1pt. The degradation
pattern is consistent with reward hacking / distribution drift when no reference
constraint is present (arxiv:2503.06639). Adding KL(π_new ‖ π_ref) with coeff=0.02
should regularize training, allowing 300 tasks (50% more data than the working case)
without regression. This is the highest-priority experiment because if it works, it
directly increases the training-data utilization of the best existing recipe.

**Estimated GPU time:** ~110 minutes on single A800 GPU (reference model doubles
peak VRAM; should still fit in 80GB).
