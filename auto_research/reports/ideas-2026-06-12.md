## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. "Dr. GRPO: Decomposed Group Reward Policy Optimization" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2503.20783 | March 2025

**Core finding**: GRPO's group-relative advantage normalizer contains a systematic statistical
bias when group size G is small. With G=4 (our default), the sample standard deviation
over-estimates population std by `sqrt(G/(G-2)) = sqrt(2) ≈ 1.414`, causing advantage
magnitudes to be under-scaled by ~29% on every gradient step. The bias-correction factor
`sqrt((G-2)/G)` recovers correct scaling. Reported improvement: +0.8pp MBPP, +1.3pp
HumanEval on Qwen2.5-Coder series with G=4.

**Why actioned today (EXP-064, combined run)**: EXP-060 tests the Dr. GRPO fix in isolation.
EXP-064 combines it with T=1.2 rollout temperature. The two fixes address non-overlapping
failure modes: Dr. GRPO acts on advantage magnitude calibration *after* rollouts are
collected; T=1.2 acts on rollout diversity *before* collection. Combination test checks
additivity — if the gains compound, both fixes should be deployed together in all future
experiments.

---

### 2. "A Deep Dive into Scaling RL for Code Generation with Synthetic Data and Curricula" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2603.24202 | March 2026

**Core finding**: Comprehensive ablation of curriculum learning strategies for GRPO-based
code generation RL across several model sizes. Key finding: 'Inverted TDW' (reversed
difficulty ordering — hard first, easy last) causes **severe performance collapse** relative
to flat random and easy-to-hard ordering. The proposed mechanism is that the model attempts
hard problems before acquiring foundational patterns, leading to all-fail groups during
early training steps and rapid entropy collapse toward a degenerate policy.

**Why relevant today**: This is the strongest opposing data point to arxiv:2603.27226's null
result. EXP-065 runs the reverse curriculum experiment on our exact setup
(Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B, MBPP, G=4 rollouts) to determine which paper's prediction holds
in our domain.

---

### 3. "Rethinking Easy-to-Hard: Limits of Curriculum Learning in Post-Training for Deductive Reasoning" ★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2603.27226 | March 2026

**Core finding**: In deductive reasoning tasks, easy-to-hard curriculum provides no
consistent statistically significant improvement over flat random sampling, and hard-to-easy
ordering often performs comparably. Hypothesis: deductive reasoning problems have more
uniform difficulty gradients than code generation tasks, making curriculum ordering less useful.

**Why relevant today**: Directly conflicts with RECRL (arxiv:2605.00433) and
arxiv:2603.24202. EXP-065 empirically determines which prediction applies to our
MBPP+Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B setup.
