## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. arxiv:2607.01490 — Don't Let Gains FADE: Breaking Down Policy Gradient Weights in RL
**Juliette Decugis, Sean O'Brien, Francis Bach, Gabriel Synnaeve, Taco Cohen**  
**Meta FAIR / Inria / ENS-PSL — July 1, 2026**

**Core framework:** Decomposes any policy gradient update into two orthogonal axes:

1. **Sign axis:** Ratio of positive-advantage mass M+ to negative-advantage mass M-.
   - **Positive dominance (M+ >> M-)**: policy update reinforces success heavily but
     barely penalizes failure → entropy collapses. Common when the model already passes
     many tasks (like our cycle 3 baseline at 92.68% router).
   - **Negative dominance (M- >> M+)**: heavy penalization with little reinforcement
     → weight geometry degenerates.
   - The paper proves both collapse modes under the policy gradient norm.

2. **Difficulty axis:** Focal weighting of samples by |A_i|.
   - Hard-problem focus sharpens gradient signal but shrinks effective sample size.
   - Optimal focus shifts from high (exploration, learn hard problems) to medium
     (exploitation, consolidate) as entropy drops over training.

**FADE method:** Self-adapting advantage that reads training dynamics at each step:
- **Sign rebalancing:** If M+/(M++M-) > r_high=0.65, scale down positive advantages.
  If < r_low=0.35, scale down negative advantages.
- **Focal weighting:** w_i = |A_i| / mean(|A|); then A_fade = A_sign_rescaled × w_i^γ.
  γ = γ_max × (H_curr / H_init)^k decays with policy entropy — natural exploration-to-exploitation schedule.

**Key results:**
- Peak pass@1 reached **20k steps earlier** than best static baseline at 7B (LiveCodeBench).
- Best accuracy-diversity trade-off across pass@1–pass@8.
- Outperforms DAPO, GRPO, sign-advantage baselines.

**Why novel vs. queue:** AVSPO (EXP-122) and EDGE-GRPO (EXP-123) fix group-level ACR
collapse. FADE targets **batch-level** positive dominance — a collapse mode active even
when ACR is normal. After ACR is fixed, batch-level entropy collapse is the next bottleneck.

### 2. arxiv:2607.04364 — RL Forgets! Towards Continual Policy Optimization
**Mao-Lin Luo et al. — July 2026**

**Core finding:** RL-based training (including GRPO) suffers severe catastrophic forgetting
during continual post-training — contrary to community belief that RL is "inherently
less prone to forgetting" than SFT.

**CPO method:** Replay-free continual RL with two loss terms:
```
L_total = L_GRPO + λ_KL · KL(π_curr || π_prev) + λ_L2 · ||θ_curr − θ_prev||²
```
- **KL term:** Behavioral constraint — keeps current policy close to prior cycle's policy
  on the current batch of on-policy prompts (no replay buffer).
- **L2 term:** Parameter-movement constraint — limits geometric drift of the adapter weights.
- λ=0.1, λ_L2=λ×0.5 by default; replay-free (only the prior adapter checkpoint in memory).

**Key results on Qwen3-VL-8B:**
- **13.7% forgetting reduction** vs. standard continual GRPO.
- **7.0% pretrained capability improvement.**

**Relevance to our pipeline:** Root Problem D — never explicitly addressed. Our 4-cycle
GRPO runs cycle N on top of cycle (N-1)'s adapter. If cycle N overwrites cycle (N-1)'s
learned behaviors, the "full arm" doesn't accumulate improvements across cycles, and
the stagnation (full = router at 92.68%) would persist even after ACR is fixed.

### 3. arxiv:2606.29238 — On the Policy Gradient Foundations of GRPO: Credit Assignment, Gradient Sparsity, and Rank Collapse
**Amritansh Mishra, Supriyo Chakraborty, Berkcan Kapusuzoglu — June 2026**

**Core finding:** GRPO's gradient matrix has effective rank ≈ 2 regardless of group
size R ∈ {2, 4, 8}. Under output-only reward, every token in a rollout receives
identical advantage → gradient collapses to a rank-2 structure (zero-sum constraint
on advantages). Additional rollouts beyond R=2 refine the direction magnitude but
add no new gradient dimensions.

**Implication for our queue:** This is a theoretical result explaining WHY group-level
interventions (AVSPO, EDGE-GRPO) improve things — they increase effective rank by
differentiating within-group advantages. We cite this paper as motivation for EXP-124
(FADE), which further differentiates across the batch via sign-axis and focal weighting.
NOT queued separately (theoretical; no new training procedure for our pipeline kind set).

### EXP-124: FADE — Focal Advantage with Dynamic Entropy
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_19_001_fade_focal_advantage_dynamic_entropy_humaneval`  
**Priority:** 8 · **Kind:** `grpo_continual` · **GPU hours:** ~3.0h

**Addresses:** Batch-level positive-dominance entropy collapse (Root Problem A extension)  
**Arxiv:** 2607.01490 (Meta FAIR / ENS-PSL, July 1, 2026)

**What changes (`grpo_train_simple.py`, ~15 lines):**
1. After GRPO advantages computed: compute M+ and M-, balance ratio r.
2. If r > 0.65: scale positive advantages by (1−r)/r (prevent entropy collapse).
3. If r < 0.35: scale negative advantages by r/(1−r) (prevent geometry collapse).
4. Per-sequence focal weight: w_i = |A_i| / mean(|A|).
5. Once per epoch: measure H_curr on 50 held-out prompts.
6. focal_gamma = FADE_FOCAL_GAMMA_MAX × (H_curr / H_init)^FADE_FOCAL_K.
7. Final: A_fade(i) = A_sign_rescaled(i) × w_i^focal_gamma.

**Env vars:** `FADE_ENABLED`, `FADE_R_HIGH=0.65`, `FADE_R_LOW=0.35`,
`FADE_FOCAL_GAMMA_MAX=1.0`, `FADE_FOCAL_K=2`, `FADE_ENTROPY_EVAL_PROMPTS=50`.

**Why now:** AVSPO and EDGE-GRPO (EXP-122/123, queued yesterday) fix group-level ACR
collapse. FADE targets a distinct, complementary failure mode: batch-level entropy
collapse from positive dominance. In our cycle 3 baseline (92.68% pass rate), the
model already passes many tasks → M+ >> M- → entropy collapse even with ACR fixed.
FADE is the only method in the queue explicitly designed for this regime. Paper reports
20k step speedup and best pass@k diversity.

**Expected outcome:** Policy entropy stabilizes above 2.0 nats throughout training;
full arm pass@1 ≥ 93.5%; pass@4 diversity maintained vs. baseline.

### EXP-125: CPO-KL — Inter-Cycle Forgetting Regularization
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_19_002_cpo_kl_intercycle_forgetting_reg_humaneval`  
**Priority:** 7 · **Kind:** `grpo_continual` · **GPU hours:** ~3.5h

**Addresses:** Root Problem D — inter-cycle catastrophic forgetting in continual GRPO  
**Arxiv:** 2607.04364 (July 2026)

**What changes (`grpo_train_simple.py`, ~25 lines):**
1. At cycle N start (N ≥ 1): load cycle (N-1) GRPO adapter as frozen π_prev.
2. Each training step, compute auxiliary losses:
   - `L_KL = mean(KL(π_curr(·|x) || π_prev(·|x)))` over current batch.
   - `L_L2 = ||θ_curr − θ_prev||² / n_params` (LoRA adapter params only).
3. `L_total = L_grpo + CPO_LAMBDA × L_KL + CPO_LAMBDA × CPO_L2_ALPHA × L_L2`.
4. Log all three losses per step.
5. Ablation sub-runs: KL-only, L2-only, combined.

**Env vars:** `CPO_KL_ENABLED`, `CPO_L2_ENABLED`, `CPO_LAMBDA=0.1`,
`CPO_L2_ALPHA=0.5`, `CPO_PRIOR_POLICY` (default: inferred from prev cycle).

**Why now:** The "RL Forgets!" paper (July 2026) is the first to demonstrate and
measure GRPO-specific forgetting in a continual multi-task setting. Our pipeline has
been stagnant at full arm = router arm across all cycles. Part of that stagnation
may be inter-cycle forgetting — each GRPO cycle erasing the previous. EXP-111
measured this diagnostically; EXP-125 is the intervention. The CPO overhead is low
(~10% extra compute from one KL forward per step; no replay buffer). If CPO breaks
the cycle stagnation, it directly addresses the paper's GRPO narrative for AAAI.
Distinct from EXP-117 (KL-only CPO, predates the L2 parameter-movement component
added by the July 2026 paper).

**Expected outcome:** Inter-cycle forgetting drops by ≥10pp on the prev-cycle task
split; full arm pass@1 shows monotone improvement across cycles (≥0.5pp/cycle);
combined KL+L2 outperforms KL-only (EXP-117) by ≥1pp.
