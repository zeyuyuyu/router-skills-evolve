## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. DEAR: Decision-Evidence Aware Reasoning Distillation ★★★
**ArXiv ID:** 2606.22830 | June 26, 2026 | Meituan Longcat Team, Nanjing University, TJUNLP Lab

**Core finding:** Standard on-policy distillation applies uniform per-token KL supervision. DEAR identifies two privileged token classes in reasoning/code generation sequences:
- **Decision tokens**: positions of high student entropy where the model must choose a structural branch (e.g., `if` vs. `while`, `return` vs. `continue`). Identified by H_t > top-20% entropy per sequence.
- **Evidence tokens**: positions near decision anchors where the teacher-student KL divergence is highest AND hidden-state cosine similarity to the nearest decision anchor is high. Identified by evidence_score = cos_sim(h_t, h_decision) × KL(t).

DEAR applies λ_decision=3.0 weight at decision positions and λ_evidence=2.0 at evidence positions. All other tokens contribute zero to the SFT loss.

**Results (Qwen2.5-1.5B ← Qwen2.5-14B):**
- HumanEval: +2.13pp pass@1 vs standard OPD
- MBPP+: **+5.74pp** pass@1 vs standard OPD (concentrated on hard tasks)
- APPS: +2.56pp pass@1 vs standard OPD

**Relevance:** Our Phase 3a SFT uses uniform token supervision on large-model traces. DEAR directly addresses this: in code generation, the student is most uncertain at control-flow choice points (decision tokens) — exactly where the large model's guidance is most valuable. The +5.74pp MBPP+ gain uses the same Qwen2.5-1.5B student and Qwen2.5-14B teacher as our setup. Implementation: ~40 lines in `train_small_model.py`. **Orthogonal** to MOPD (EXP-109: peer context) and DemoPSD (EXP-107: trace selection). Motivated **EXP-110**.

### 2. RouteNLP: Closed-Loop LLM Routing with Conformal Cascading and Distillation Co-Optimization ★★★
**ArXiv ID:** 2604.23577 | April 26, 2026 | Dongxin Guo, Jikun Wu, Siu Ming Yiu — HKU / Stellaris AI

**Core finding:** Standard LLM routing treats the model portfolio as fixed — train router once, deploy, never retrain. RouteNLP proposes a closed-loop framework:
1. Route queries through the cascade (small → large if small fails).
2. Log all escalations (queries sent to large model).
3. Cluster escalation logs by query embedding to identify failure modes.
4. Apply **targeted distillation** to the small model on those failure clusters (large-model traces).
5. Recalibrate router confidence thresholds via conformal prediction.

The key insight: the router's escalation decisions are a perfect oracle for which tasks the small model cannot currently handle — those exact tasks are the highest-value distillation targets.

**Results:** >2× cost reduction at equal quality vs. untargeted distillation across 4 routing benchmarks.

**Relevance:** Our cycle-3 router escalates 6.10% of tasks (~5 tasks out of 82 HumanEval). Those ~5 escalated tasks are exactly the hardest ones the small model currently fails. Our Phase 3a SFT currently applies uniform distillation weight to all 82 tasks — giving the same weight to tasks the router confidently routes to small (already solved) as to the escalated failure cases. RouteNLP prescribes upweighting the escalated tasks (weight=5.0) in the SFT DataLoader. Implementation: ~15 lines in `traces_to_sft.py`. Closes the skills-router co-evolution loop: router trains ON small model failures → router decisions GUIDE small model's next SFT. **Motivated EXP-111.**

### 3. On the Position Bias of On-Policy Distillation ★★ (Diagnostic)
**ArXiv ID:** 2606.22600 | June 2026

**Core finding:** OPD exhibits systematic position bias: the KL loss at later token positions is numerically larger than at earlier positions, causing the training signal to over-represent the tail of sequences (closing brackets, final `return` statements in code) and under-represent critical early structural choices. This bias persists even with standard per-token normalization.

**Relevance:** Directly explains part of our skills arm gap. In HumanEval code generation, the critical structural decisions (function signature, algorithm choice, loop structure) appear in the first 20-40% of the sequence — exactly the positions that OPD's position bias under-weights. DEAR (EXP-110) indirectly addresses this via entropy-based decision token identification, which tends to select early structural tokens. A dedicated position-bias correction (upweight first-half tokens) could complement DEAR. Not a new experiment today — background insight that reinforces DEAR's value.

**ID:** `exp_2026_07_06_001_dear_decision_evidence_token_selective_sft_humaneval`
**Kind:** `joint_cycle_multiseed` | **Priority:** 7 | **Estimated GPU:** 1.5h

**Motivation:** arxiv:2606.22830 (June 26, 2026). Our Phase 3a SFT uses token-uniform KL supervision. DEAR identifies that code generation has two privileged token classes: decision tokens (high student entropy = control-flow choice points) and evidence tokens (near-decision positions with high teacher-student KL). Applying 3× weight at decision tokens and 2× at evidence tokens — and 0 elsewhere — concentrates the training signal on structurally critical positions. The paper reports +2.13pp HumanEval and +5.74pp MBPP for the same 1.5B Qwen student model. This is orthogonal to MOPD (peer context, EXP-109), DemoPSD (trace selection, EXP-107), and RouteNLP (sample weighting, EXP-111) — all four can be combined.

**Spec:** 1-cycle HumanEval, skills arm only (no GRPO). In `train_small_model.py`: after student forward pass, compute per-token entropy H_t; identify top-20% entropy positions as decision tokens; compute evidence scores as cos_sim(h_t, h_decision) × KL(t) for non-decision tokens; identify top-20% evidence positions. Loss: 3.0×KL at decision + 2.0×KL at evidence + 0 elsewhere. Three variants: (A) DEAR (decision + evidence), (B) decision-only, (C) standard OPD baseline (75.61%). Metrics: per-arm pass@1, decision/evidence token fraction, hard-task vs easy-task breakdown.

**Target:** Skills arm pass@1 ≥ 77.74% (75.61% + 2.13pp DEAR paper's HumanEval gain). Contrastive with variant (B): evidence supervision adds measurable value beyond decision supervision alone. If pass@1 ≥ 78.5%: skills gap reduces to 17.8pp from 20.7pp — strong narrative for AAAI §4.
