## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. Group-Graph Policy Optimization for Long-Horizon Agentic Reinforcement Learning
**arxiv:2606.22995** · June 22, 2026

**What it does:** G2PO transforms multi-turn RL trajectories from isolated linear
sequences into a global state-transition graph. Two core innovations:

**(a) Group-Aggregation State-Value Estimation**: Instead of estimating V(s_t) from
a single rollout's return, G2PO identifies all rollouts in the batch that visit
"equivalent" states (same observation content, possibly across different tasks) and
pools their value estimates. This reduces the effective variance of the value baseline
by increasing the "virtual group size" from G=8 to potentially G_effective=30–50 for
frequently-visited intermediate states.

**(b) Edge-Centric Advantage Estimation**: G2PO redefines advantages at the level of
state-transition edges (s_t, a_t, s_{t+1}) rather than at the rollout level. TD errors
δ(e) = R_final + V_agg(s_{t+1}) - V_agg(s_t) are computed for every edge in the batch
and globally standardized. This identifies the specific turn-transitions that most
drive task progress, concentrating gradient on critical decisions regardless of which
rollout or task they came from.

**Evaluation:** WebShop (+11% success), ALFWorld (+8%), AppWorld (+14% vs. step-level
GRPO). Particularly effective when many rollouts share intermediate states.

**Why relevant:** Our tau2 multi-turn repair setting is a textbook G2PO use case:
- Up to 3 turns per task, with ~30% of tasks reaching turn 2
- Within a group of G=8 rollouts for one task, all start from the same prompt (identical
  turn-0 observation) → value aggregation gives exact pooling across G rollouts
- Across tasks in the batch, ~12–15 tasks often produce similar "boundary-check repair"
  or "type-cast repair" turn-2 patterns → cross-task pooling reduces baseline variance

**Distinctness:** Not cited in any prior pending queue update. G2PO's graph aggregation
is orthogonal to EXP-091 (TRUST's per-turn entropy weighting) — they can be composed.

### 2. MDP-GRPO: Stabilized Group Relative Policy Optimization for Multi-Constraint Instruction Following
**arxiv:2606.06058** · June 4, 2026

**What it does:** Extends GRPO to handle K simultaneous reward signals without gradient
interference. Key observation: when two reward objectives have non-aligned gradient
directions (cosine angle < 0), standard scalar-weighted combination causes oscillation.
MDP-GRPO maintains K separate advantage estimates, normalizes each per-constraint to
equalize variance, then applies a feasibility projection: if gradient g_k of constraint k
"points away from" the combined gradient direction, project g_k onto the feasible cone.
This guarantees first-order joint improvement on all constraints when feasible.

**Evaluation:** 11% improvement on multi-constraint instruction-following benchmark vs.
vanilla GRPO; gradient oscillation reduced by 67%.

**Why relevant:** Our grpo_continual runs optimize a single HumanEval reward signal and
have never measured or controlled MBPP forgetting. Given 4 cycles of HumanEval
specialization with G=8 DAPO rollouts, the model likely forgets general coding patterns
(expected MBPP degradation ≥5 pp based on standard continual RL literature). MDP-GRPO
offers a principled way to interleave anti-forgetting gradient signal (MBPP rollouts
every 20 steps) without adding a separate replay phase that shifts the training
distribution.

**Distinctness:** EXP-087 (FOREVER) applies Ebbinghaus-curve replay in the SFT phase.
EXP-078 (on-policy replay) uses MBPP in a post-GRPO replay phase. MDP-GRPO integrates
the anti-forgetting constraint within the GRPO objective itself, avoiding the
train/eval distribution gap introduced by separate phases.

### 3. Cascadia: An Efficient Cascade Serving System for Large Language Models
**arxiv:2506.04203** · June 2026

**What it does:** Proposes a serving system that dynamically decides whether to serve a
query with a small model or cascade to a large model, based on both query-level features
(difficulty estimate from embedding) AND system-level features (current GPU load, latency
SLA budget). Introduces a cascade-aware SLA-respecting routing policy trained with
reinforcement learning.

**Why partially relevant:** Our router currently trains only on query-level features
(raw prompt embedding) without any system-level or latency-budget features. Cascadia
suggests that routing accuracy could be improved by incorporating dynamic system signals.
However, this requires serving infrastructure changes beyond a simple GRPO modification.

### EXP-092: G2PO Graph-Structured Credit Assignment for tau2 Multi-Turn GRPO
**ID:** `exp_2026_06_27_001_g2po_graph_credit_tau2_multiturn`
**Kind:** `joint_cycle_multiseed`  **Priority:** 8  **Est. GPU-hours:** 3.0

**Rationale (short):** Standard GRPO assigns trajectory-level advantage from G=8
rollouts per task. In tau2's multi-turn repair setting, this gives a noisy baseline
(small group) and ignores structural similarity across tasks (multiple tasks often arrive
at similar turn-2 repair patterns). G2PO builds a global state-transition graph across
the batch, pools value estimates across equivalent states (same task group AND similar
cross-task turn content), and computes edge-centric TD-error advantages globally
normalized across all batch transitions. Validated on WebShop (+11%), ALFWorld (+8%),
AppWorld (+14%) vs. step-level GRPO. Implementation ~40 lines in `grpo_train_simple.py`
using already-computed rollout tokens (no extra forward passes).

**Config:** tau2_bench, Qwen3-235B-A22B, 4 cycles × 2 seeds {42, 7}, G=8, max 3 turns,
Jaccard threshold 0.65 for cross-task node merging.

**Target:** tau2 task_pass > 72.97% (cycle-3 router reference), large_model_fraction < 35.5%.

### EXP-093: MDP-GRPO Anti-Forgetting λ Staircase (HumanEval + MBPP)
**ID:** `exp_2026_06_27_002_mdpgrpo_multiseed_staircase_antiforgetting`
**Kind:** `grpo_multi_seed_staircase`  **Priority:** 7  **Est. GPU-hours:** 3.0

**Rationale (short):** Four cycles of HumanEval GRPO almost certainly cause MBPP
forgetting (expected ≥5 pp degradation, never measured). MDP-GRPO adds an anti-forgetting
constraint (λ_af × L_GRPO_mbpp) with feasibility projection to prevent gradient
interference when HE and MBPP gradients oppose. Staircase: seed 42 (λ_af=0.0 baseline),
seed 7 (λ_af=0.2 light), seed 13 (λ_af=0.5 strong). 1-cycle run each; identifies the
Pareto frontier between specialization and general-coding preservation.

**Config:** humaneval bench, Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B-Instruct, 1 cycle × 3 seeds,
MBPP interleaved every 20 steps (10 tasks × G=4), feasibility projection enabled.

**Target:** At λ_af=0.2: HE pass@1 ≥ 82%, MBPP forgetting ≤ 2 pp vs. pre-training.
At λ_af=0.0: MBPP forgetting ≥ 5 pp (confirms the risk). Informs optimal λ for
future 4-cycle runs.
