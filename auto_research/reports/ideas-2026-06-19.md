## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. "Improving LLM Code Generation via Requirement-Aware Curriculum Reinforcement Learning" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2605.00433 | May 2026 | (ICML 2026 submission)

**Core finding**: Standard GRPO trains on a static sample of coding problems, causing
wasted gradient capacity on problems the model already masters (all-pass) or cannot solve
at any temperature (all-fail). RECRL automatically estimates per-problem difficulty from
in-epoch rollout statistics and constructs training batches that oversample the
"Goldilocks zone" (pass rate ∈ [0.2, 0.8]) and undersample the extremes. Experiments on
Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B (the exact model in this project) achieve +3.1 pp Pass@1 on
HumanEval versus flat-sampling GRPO, and +3.9 pp on MBPP — meaningfully larger than the
+1.8/+2.3 pp gains from flat GRPO.

**Relevance to this project**: The grpo_info.json from cycle 3 of e2e_4cyc_gpt55 shows
52.4% zero-variance groups at G=8 DAPO (43/82 problems). RECRL attacks this at the
sampling stage (before generation), while EXP-077 (G=16 DDT) attacks it at the
rollout-aggregation stage (after generation). The two are complementary and their
interaction is a publishable ablation for AAAI 2027.

**Actioned**: EXP-078 (`grpo_curriculum_continual`, priority 7)

### 2. "On-Policy Replay for Continual Supervised Fine-Tuning" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2605.29495 | June 2026 | ICML 2026 Workshop on LifeLong Learning

**Core finding**: In continual fine-tuning, off-policy rehearsal (replaying stored
historical outputs) creates distribution mismatch as the model evolves. On-Policy Replay
(OPR) instead rolls out the *current* checkpoint on a small budget of historical prompts
(5-10% of the historical dataset), filters outputs by a task reward function, and replays
the surviving (prompt, model_output) pairs as SFT loss terms in the next epoch. The key
insight: on-policy outputs stay on the current model's distribution, preventing the KL
divergence blow-up that causes off-policy rehearsal to fail. OPR with 5% prompt budget
matches EWC with 50% budget and outperforms DER++ (dark experience replay) at equal
compute.

**Relevance to this project**: Two independent signals suggest cross-benchmark forgetting
is a real risk here: (1) EXP-076 was queued as a priority-9 forgetting evaluation; (2)
cwy_35b_fullsplit_20260610 shows the Full arm *declining* across multi-cycle tau2 SFT
(70.22% → 69.66%). EXP-079 is the proactive fix: add 30 MBPP tasks × 4 OPR rollouts
per epoch to the HumanEval GRPO loop, filter survivors by pytest, replay at 20% mix.
Wall-clock overhead: ~96 s per epoch (negligible vs. 90-min main GRPO loop).

**Actioned**: EXP-079 (`grpo_continual`, priority 8)

### 3. "Posterior-GRPO: Rewarding Reasoning Processes in Code Generation" ★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2508.05170 | August 2025

**Core finding**: Instead of binary outcome reward (pass/fail tests), Posterior-GRPO
computes advantage using the posterior probability of a correct reasoning process given
the final answer. This provides denser gradient signal in the intermediate token space
and achieves +4.7 pp Pass@1 on HumanEval over standard GRPO.

**Relevance and deferral**: The mechanism requires process-level labels (step-by-step
reasoning annotation or a learned process reward model). Our reward is binary pytest
pass/fail and there are no intermediate steps in the current multi-turn repair setup that
can be labeled as "reasoning traces." Implementing Posterior-GRPO would require adding a
process reward model (PRF), which is a non-trivial 1-2 week engineering effort on top of
the existing pipeline. Deferred pending A800 restoration and bandwidth.
