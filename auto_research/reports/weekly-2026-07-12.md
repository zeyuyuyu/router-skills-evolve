### 2. Two New Mechanisms Queued for the Zero-Variance Bottleneck (ACR = 52.4%)

**EXP-112 — Forward-KL teacher distillation** (arxiv:2605.12483):
Replacing hard-label SFT in Phase 3a with forward-KL distillation from K=8 GPT-5.5 traces
targets the 20.7pp skills gap from a different angle than GRPO variants. The paper shows
that for student models, dense token-level teacher supervision outperforms sparse reward
training on hard tasks — exactly the ACR=52.4% regime. Predicted: skills arm +2–3pp
(75.61% → ≥78%), ACR reduction from 52.4% to ≤45%. Both positive and negative
results are publishable (negative rules out distillation quality as the explanation,
focusing the gap explanation on hard-task ceiling).

**EXP-113 — RECRL + sign-advantage composition**:
Proactive mechanism (RECRL: prevents zero-variance groups via curriculum) + reactive
mechanism (sign-advantage: provides A=2r−1 gradient for remaining all-fail groups).
Non-overlapping code regions (~22 lines total change). Theoretically supraadditive in
the hard-task difficulty regime (difficulty > 0.7). If EXP-108 and EXP-110 are
individually positive, EXP-113 measures whether their combination is supraadditive.

### 3. Forgetting Eval Opens a New Paper Section (EXP-111)

EXP-111 is eval-only (0.5 GPU-hours): compare cycle-0 vs cycle-3 checkpoints on tasks
the skills arm solved in cycle 0. arxiv:2507.05386 predicts 8–15% SFT forgetting vs
<3% GRPO/RFT forgetting. If confirmed, forgetting explains part of the 20.7pp skills gap
(75.61% vs 96.34%) and provides a new §5.x "Forgetting Analysis" subsection — a concrete
mechanistic narrative strengthening the paper's argument for GRPO-over-SFT in multi-cycle
evolution.
