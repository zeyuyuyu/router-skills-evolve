## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. "No Prompt Left Behind: Exploiting Zero-Variance Prompts in LLM RL via Entropy-Guided Advantage Shaping" ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ
**ArXiv ID**: [2509.21880](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.21880) | Sep 2025 preprint | **ICLR 2026**

**Core finding**: Standard GRPO/DAPO discards zero-variance groups (all-pass or all-fail) because the group-relative advantage collapses to zero, providing no gradient signal. RL-ZVP proposes entropy-guided advantage shaping that recaptures these groups:

- **All-pass groups**: Assign positive advantage `A = +Œ± √ó H(token)` per token. High-entropy tokens (the model was uncertain but succeeded) receive the strongest positive signal ‚Äî reinforcing the uncertain-but-correct policy path without over-reinforcing already-peaked tokens.
- **All-fail groups**: Assign negative advantage `A = -Œ± √ó H(token)` per token. High-entropy tokens (uncertain AND wrong) receive the strongest penalty, steering the policy away from the failure mode.
- **Mixed groups**: Standard DAPO group-relative advantage (unchanged).

**Results**: +8.61 pp on MATH500, +7.77 pp average across 6 math reasoning benchmarks with Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct. Published at ICLR 2026.

**Why actioned today as EXP-082** (priority 8):
- Our grpo_info.json shows 43/82 = 52.4% zero-variance groups are discarded per epoch at G=8 DAPO
- EXP-077 (DDT) and EXP-078 (RECRL) address this via different mechanisms (temperature adaptation, pre-generation curriculum) ‚Äî RL-ZVP is orthogonal (post-rollout signal recapture)
- The 43 discarded groups √ó 8 rollouts = 344 completions are already generated but provide zero gradient; RL-ZVP recaptures all 344 at zero additional inference cost
- Implementation: ~25 lines in grpo_train_simple.py replacing the DAPO zero-variance discard with entropy-scaled advantage assignment
- No queued experiment uses entropy-guided advantage shaping ‚Äî genuinely novel addition

**Estimated wall-clock**: ~90 min on 1√ó A800 80GB (Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B, G=8, 1 epoch, HumanEval 82 tasks ‚Äî now all 82 groups processed vs 39 in reference)

### 2. "Mechanistic Origins of Catastrophic Forgetting: Why RL Preserves Circuits Better than SFT?" ‚òÖ‚òÖ
**ArXiv ID**: [2605.28860](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28860) | June 6, 2026 | Rojas Nunez et al.

**Core finding**: RL fine-tuning preserves a larger fraction of the model's base computational circuits (measured via differential circuit vulnerability at the attention-head level) compared to SFT. SFT adapts faster to the target task but disrupts significantly more circuits, explaining catastrophic forgetting. RL adapts more slowly but preserves prior capability pathways.

**Relevance to our project**:
- The tau2 multi-cycle decline (70.22%‚Üí72.47%‚Üí70.22%‚Üí69.66% fullsplit) involves both SFT phases (Phase 3a) and GRPO phases (Phase 3b) per cycle. arxiv:2605.28860 predicts that the SFT phases are the primary source of forgetting, not GRPO.
- This suggests a targeted diagnostic: run tau2 with `SKIP_GRPO=1` (SFT-only pipeline) across 2 cycles and compare to the existing joint run. If SFT-only also declines, it confirms SFT forgetting. If SFT-only holds or improves, GRPO is the culprit.

**Why NOT actioned today**: The 2-cycle tau2 SFT-only diagnostic would take ~3.5 GPU-hours, and the queue is already at 83 pending. Deferred to a future session with lower backlog, or as a companion to EXP-066 (tau2 router regularization) once A800 is restored. Short-term paper fix: add a sentence in ¬ß7 noting that arxiv:2605.28860 predicts GRPO naturally preserves circuits better than SFT, suggesting the tau2 cycle decline (W6) is SFT-induced, consistent with the on-policy replay literature (arxiv:2605.29495 / EXP-079).

### 3. "Towards Efficient Multi-LLM Inference: Characterization and Analysis of LLM Routing and Hierarchical Techniques" ‚òÖ‚òÖ
**ArXiv ID**: [2506.06579](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.06579) | June 2025

**Core finding**: Deployment-aware taxonomy of LLM routing techniques (task-based, cost-based, quality-based, cascade). Documents that **random-proportion routing** (Bernoulli-p policy) is the standard minimum required baseline in routing evaluation: without it, one cannot distinguish "the router learned useful task-routing" from "the router happens to use the cheaper model a lot". Introduces Inference Efficiency Score (IES) as a unified cost-performance metric.

**Why actioned today as EXP-083** (priority 7):
- Paper review W5 explicitly flags: "No evaluation of FrugalGPT-style cascade, random-50% baseline, or MoA on tau2 tasks"
- The tau2 held-out results make this urgent: always-large = 60.11% < always-small = 76.97%. Any policy that calls large at all will degrade performance. The router (69.66%) is doing better than random-50% (~68.5%) and much better than always-large ‚Äî but the paper doesn't show this comparison.
- A random-50% and random-10% baseline (matching router fallback rate ~1.1%) take <30 min on A800 using existing traces ‚Äî no new model calls needed for the small-model arm; large-model re-calls needed only for the small fraction of tasks randomly assigned to large.
- This baseline is uniquely cheap and uniquely high-value for AAAI soundness.
