## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. Beyond GRPO and On-Policy Distillation: Sparse-to-Dense Reward Principle ★★★
**ArXiv ID:** 2605.12483 | May 12, 2026 | Under review

**Core finding:** The central insight is a reward-density allocation principle: sparse sequence-level reward (GRPO) is most informative on models with enough exploration capacity to discover improvements, while dense token-level teacher supervision (forward-KL distillation) is better for compressing a strong teacher's behavior into a smaller student. The paper proposes a four-stage workflow — teacher RL → KL warmup → on-policy distillation → optional student RL — and demonstrates that an RL-improved 8B teacher distilled via forward-KL into a 3B student outperforms direct GRPO on the 3B student across GSM8K, MATH, MBPP, HumanEval, and LiveCodeBench. The gain is largest on hard tasks where the 3B model's pass_rate under direct GRPO is near zero — exactly the ACR=52.4% regime.

**Relevance to us:** Our current Phase 3a trains the small model with hard-label SFT (cross-entropy on teacher argmax). This discards the teacher's soft token distribution — if GPT-5.5 places probability mass on equivalent but stylistically different solutions, SFT binarizes this to zero. Forward-KL distillation transfers the full teacher distribution using empirical token frequencies from the K=8 GPT-5.5 traces already collected by SCALING_FORCE_BOTH=1. This could improve the skills arm (richer code patterns → higher pass@1 even without GRPO) and reduce ACR (better student initialization → some hard tasks become mixed-variance in Phase 3b GRPO). Not previously proposed; distinct from VeRPO (reward shaping), EGCA (credit assignment), and sign advantage (advantage formula). Motivated **EXP-112**.

### 2. RECRL × Sign Advantage Composition ★★★
**ArXiv IDs:** 2605.00433 (RECRL) × 2605.07689 (Sign Advantage) | Referenced in EXP-110 and EXP-108 respectively

**Why compose now:** The composition was flagged as the "natural EXP-112" in the 2026-07-11 ideas report. The two mechanisms are non-overlapping in code (different functions in grpo_train_simple.py) and target the ACR=52.4% problem from complementary angles:
- **RECRL**: proactive, reduces the *frequency* of zero-variance groups by curriculum ordering (easy→hard across epochs). Epoch 1 is predicted to have ≤15% zero-variance groups vs 52.4% baseline.
- **Sign advantage**: reactive, ensures zero-variance groups *still contribute gradient* (A = 2r−1; all-fail groups get A=−1, pushing away from wrong patterns).

**Predicted behavior of composition:** RECRL concentrates gradient from tractable tasks in early epochs; sign advantage provides learning signal from hard all-fail tasks in epoch 3. RECRL's gain zone is medium-difficulty tasks (0.3 < difficulty < 0.7); sign advantage's gain zone is hard all-fail tasks (difficulty > 0.7). The two are complementary in difficulty space. If each adds ~+1pp independently, composition could add ~+2.5pp (partially supraadditive). Motivated **EXP-113**.

### 3. Signal Reshaping for GRPO in Weak-Feedback Agentic Code Repair ★★ (Reference)
**ArXiv ID:** 2605.07276 | May 2026

**Core finding:** GRPO's within-group comparison is only semantically valid after three kinds of signal are reshaped: (1) outcome rewards recover semantic ranking (execution pass rate, not compiler success), (2) process scores localize intra-trajectory credit, (3) rollout comparability ensures all G rollouts face identical execution conditions. Full signal reshaping improves strict accuracy from 0.385 to 0.535 on agentic compile-fix; process-score weighting reduces average evaluation steps from 23.50 to 17.02.

**Relevance to us:** Component 3 (rollout comparability) is unexplored: a subset of our 43 zero-variance groups may be "false zero-variance" due to flaky tests or timeouts rather than model failure. If 10–15% of the 43 zero-variance groups are timeout-contaminated, fixing execution comparability (deterministic test timeout, consistent test environment) could reduce ACR from 52.4% to ~45% with zero training cost. Components 1 and 2 are partly covered by VeRPO (partial test-case pass rates) and EGCA (execution-grounded credit). Not queued as a new experiment today (would overlap with existing ones and requires a diagnostic first); filed for consideration if A800 restores and rollout-comparability data becomes available.
