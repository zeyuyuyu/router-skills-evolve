## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. Reinforcement Fine-Tuning Naturally Mitigates Forgetting in Continual Post-Training
**arxiv:2507.05386** | July 2026

Systematically compares SFT vs. RFT (GRPO) across 7 multimodal continual learning tasks. The central finding: **SFT leads to catastrophic forgetting; RFT inherently preserves prior task knowledge and matches multi-task training without any explicit replay**.

**Mechanism**: RFT's implicit regularization is theoretically grounded — parameter updates are conservative in subspaces sensitive to prior tasks, with this conservatism **naturally scaled by the variance of the reward signal**. The KL penalty and CoT mechanisms contribute but are not the primary driver; the variance-scaling is.

**Direct implication for our Full=Router problem**: In our ACR=52.4% setting, 43/82 GRPO groups have zero reward variance → their conservatism is maximal → gradient=0 → model unchanged for those task subspaces. These groups contribute **nothing** to training (neither learning nor forgetting). The 47.6% non-collapsed groups are where all GRPO learning happens, but their signal may not be sufficient to move aggregate pass@1 above the router baseline. This paper's theory predicts that **selectively skipping zero-variance groups** would be nearly equivalent to standard GRPO (since they contribute zero gradient anyway), but might reveal whether the non-collapsed group signal alone can push Full > Router when concentrated. Motivated **EXP-104**.

### 2. Geometric-Mean Policy Optimization (GMPO)
**arxiv:2507.20673** | Yuzhong Zhao, Yue Liu, Junpeng Liu et al. | July 2025

GMPO identifies that standard GRPO's arithmetic mean of token-level importance-weighted rewards is dominated by outlier tokens (extreme importance ratios |π_θ/π_ref|). It replaces the arithmetic mean with a **geometric mean**: instead of `mean(r_t * A)`, compute `exp(mean(log(r_t * A)))` for positive-advantage rollouts, with sign preserved for negative-advantage rollouts.

The geometric mean is inherently less sensitive to outliers — a token with importance ratio r=10 contributes log(10)=2.3 to the log-mean rather than 10 to the linear mean, bounding its influence. Tested on Qwen-7B, GMPO improves over GRPO by **+4.1% on math benchmarks** (AIME24, AMC, MATH500, OlympiadBench) and **+1.4% on multimodal reasoning**.

**Relevance**: Our HumanEval code generation has a specific outlier pattern: syntax-critical tokens (`return`, indentation, closing brackets) often differ between passing and failing rollouts precisely where correctness is decided. These critical divergences create extreme importance ratios under arithmetic-mean GRPO, causing gradient spikes on individual tokens rather than global policy improvement. GMPO's geometric mean bounds this. This is **orthogonal to ACR mitigation** — it targets the 47.6% of non-collapsed groups where GRPO IS receiving signal, aiming to make those updates more stable and effective. Motivated **EXP-105**.

### 3. Efficient Routing of Inference Requests across LLM Instances in Cloud-Edge Computing
**arxiv:2507.15553** | July 2026

Formulates LLM inference routing as a multi-objective optimization (quality, latency, cost) across heterogeneous cloud-edge instances, using NSGA-II. While primarily an infrastructure paper, its key insight relevant to us: **routing under uncertainty (where model capability varies by prompt type) benefits from explicit uncertainty quantification** rather than single-point accuracy estimates.

**Relevance**: Our logistic router outputs a probability but is trained to binary labels — it lacks calibrated uncertainty. This complements the already-queued DARS experiment (EXP-103, soft Brier score labels) and supports the case for uncertainty-aware routing. Background literature for AAAI routing ablation section.

### EXP-104: Selective-Group GRPO — Zero-Variance Group Masking (HumanEval)
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_03_001_selective_group_grpo_rft_forgetting_humaneval`
**Kind:** `grpo_continual` | **Priority:** 7 | **Estimated GPU:** 1.5h

**Motivation:** arxiv:2507.05386. Per the paper's variance-scaling theory, zero-variance GRPO groups (ACR=52.4%) contribute exactly zero gradient to training. They are pure compute waste. This experiment tests whether selectively masking these groups (3-line code change in `grpo_train_simple.py`) reveals a cleaner signal from the 47.6% of non-collapsed groups, potentially pushing Full > Router for the first time.

**Spec:** 1-cycle DAPO on HumanEval, Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B, G=8, SCALING_FORCE_BOTH=1. Rollouts collected once, re-used across three variants: (A) mask zero-variance groups, (B) mask all-pass groups only, (C) standard DAPO baseline. Selective mask implemented as `active_mask = (reward_var > 1e-6).float()` applied to loss before backward.

**Target:** If variant (A) ≥ DAPO baseline pass@1: zero-variance groups were noise (confirms paper's theory). If variant (A) > DAPO baseline: freed gradient budget improves non-collapsed group learning. Ultimate target: pass@1 > 0.93 (Full > Router for AAAI Table 2).

### EXP-105: GMPO Geometric-Mean Policy Optimization (HumanEval)
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_03_002_gmpo_geometric_mean_policy_optimization_humaneval`
**Kind:** `grpo_continual` | **Priority:** 6 | **Estimated GPU:** 1.5h

**Motivation:** arxiv:2507.20673. HumanEval code generation has outlier-prone importance ratios at syntax-critical token positions. Standard DAPO's arithmetic-mean token aggregation is dominated by these outliers. GMPO's geometric mean bounds their influence via log-mean. This is orthogonal to all queued ACR mitigations (which target zero-variance groups) — GMPO stabilizes the 47.6% of non-collapsed groups that actually carry GRPO signal.

**Spec:** 1-cycle GMPO on HumanEval, Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B, G=8, SCALING_FORCE_BOTH=1. Drop-in replacement of DAPO loss aggregation: replace `(ratio * advantages).mean(dim=-1)` with `exp((log_ratios + log_advantages).mean(dim=-1))` (sign-preserving, clipped at ε=0.2, log-stabilized at ε=1e-8). Same hyperparams as baseline otherwise.

**Target:** HE pass@1 ≥ 0.93, gradient norm variance reduced vs. DAPO baseline, importance ratio P99 reduced. Paper reports +4.1% on math; code generation outlier pattern suggests +1-3% pass@1.
