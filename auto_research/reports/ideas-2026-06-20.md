## Top 3 Relevant Analysis Threads

### 1. Statistical Uncertainty in the MBPP GRPO Result ★★★
**Source**: `results/rl_15b_a800_20260509_summary.json` (internal)

**Core finding**: The canonical MBPP GRPO result (47→49 pass@1, +2pp) is derived from
a single training run evaluated once on 100 tasks. At n=100 and p̂=0.49, the binomial
standard error is σ = sqrt(p̂(1-p̂)/n) = sqrt(0.49 × 0.51 / 100) ≈ 0.050. The 95%
Wald confidence interval is approximately [0.39, 0.59] — a width of **±10 percentage
points**. The observed gain of +2pp is smaller than σ. This means a reviewer can
correctly state: "the result is consistent with zero improvement." An AAAI reviewer
looking at a 47→49 claim in a main paper table will immediately ask for error bars
or a significance test. Without k≥3 seeds and/or larger eval sets, the paper cannot
defend this claim under scrutiny.

**Actioned**: EXP-080 (`grpo_multi_seed_staircase`, priority 9)

### 2. Non-Monotone Routing Accuracy Across Cycles ★★★
**Source**: `results/e2e_4cyc_gpt55/cycle_{0,1,2,3}/e2e_ablation_summary.json` (internal)

**Core finding**: The routing accuracy across the 4 e2e cycles is:
- Cycle 0: 91.46%
- Cycle 1: 90.24% ← DECREASED from cycle 0
- Cycle 2: 93.90% ← peak
- Cycle 3: 92.68% ← decreased from cycle 2, reported as "best"

The non-monotone trajectory is inconsistent with the paper's claim that "routing accuracy
improves over training cycles." In a single-seed run, it is impossible to determine whether
this trajectory reflects: (a) genuine non-monotone dynamics; (b) router training noise
(logistic regression is sensitive to random weight initialization even with scikit-learn
defaults); or (c) coincidental variance in the 82-task evaluation set. The cycle 1 dip is
particularly concerning for the AAAI narrative. Multi-seed router training on existing
adapters would immediately reveal whether the dip is real (reproducible across seeds) or
noise (high std at cycle 1 would explain it).

**Actioned**: EXP-081 (`joint_cycle_multiseed`, priority 8)

### 3. The `grpo_multi_seed_staircase` and `joint_cycle_multiseed` Kinds Are Unused ★★★
**Source**: full review of EXP-001 through EXP-079 in pending_queue_update chain

**Core finding**: These two kinds were defined in `runner.py` specifically for
statistical validation. Every single queued experiment (EXP-001 through EXP-079) is
a single-seed run. With the AAAI deadline 56 days away, this is a structural gap
in the experimental plan. The paper's two main claims — (1) GRPO improves MBPP from

require multi-seed validation to survive peer review. The window to run these experiments

proofread (~2 days), leaving a buffer of ~44 days before the August 15 deadline. Running
