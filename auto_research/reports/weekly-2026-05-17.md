## Headline Findings

### 1. Learned router is the system's strongest result

BERT-base classifier at threshold=0.57 achieves **93.04% routing accuracy** on 848-example eval split — a +24.8 pp improvement over always-small-fallback (68.3%) and +23.6 pp over SkillBook-signature-only routing (69.5%). Large-class F1 = 0.895; fallback rate drops to **2.1%**, meaning 97.9% of requests can be served locally without sacrificing accuracy.

### 2. GRPO at 1.5B, 200 tasks is the only confirmed positive LLM training result

Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B-Instruct with GRPO (200 tasks × 4 rollouts, LoRA r=16, lr=5e-6, qwen-chat) improves from **47/100 → 49/100** on MBPP eval@100 (+2 pp, +4.3% relative). The same recipe fails at 3B (−2 pp), 7B (−2 pp), and at 1.5B with 400 tasks (−1 pp). SFT in all tested configurations fails or degrades.

### 3. Task-count is nonmonotonic — 200 tasks is the current ceiling

1.5B GRPO at 200 tasks: +2 pp. At 400 tasks: −1 pp. More data **hurts** under the flat-GRPO recipe. This constrains all future training designs and motivates curriculum ordering + KL regularization experiments in the 13-experiment pending queue.

### Priority 2: Run KL + curriculum combined experiment (highest-priority training run)
`exp_2026_05_17_001` (KL-coeff=0.02 + easy-to-hard curriculum, 300 tasks, 1.5B):
- Addresses two orthogonal failure modes simultaneously
- KL prevents distribution collapse; curriculum prevents zero-gradient on hard tasks
- Expected gain: +4–6 pp if additive (each individually predicted +2–3 pp)
- This is the most likely path to a paper-quality LLM result (≥5 pp gain)

### Priority 3: Multi-seed verification of the +2 pp result

- Three seeds gives error bars needed for a credible paper claim
