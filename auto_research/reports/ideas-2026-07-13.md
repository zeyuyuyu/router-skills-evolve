## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. RLCSD: Reinforcement Learning with Contrastive On-Policy Self-Distillation ★★★
**ArXiv ID:** 2606.11709 | June 11, 2026 | Tsinghua / Alibaba Tongyi Lab | Under review

**Core finding:** Standard on-policy self-distillation (OPSD) methods generate dense token-level supervision by comparing the model's distribution to its own outputs under a "privileged context" (a verified correct solution hint). However, the learning signal concentrates on *style tokens* (indentation, length, formatting — which the hint changes regardless of correctness) rather than *task-bearing tokens* (the reasoning steps that actually differ between correct and incorrect solutions). This "privilege-induced style drift" destabilizes training and can cause response length collapse.

RLCSD's fix is contrastive: compute the distributional gap under a *correct* hint AND under a *wrong* hint. Both induce the same style drift; their difference cancels the style component and concentrates gradient on the reasoning-critical token positions. Formally: `L_RLCSD = L_GRPO + λ·[KL(M(·|x,correct_hint)||M_θ) - KL(M(·|x,wrong_hint)||M_θ)]`. Experiments on Qwen3 (1.7B/4B/8B) and OLMo-3-7B-Think across math and logic benchmarks show consistent improvements over GRPO and prior OPSD methods.

**Relevance to us:** Our ACR=52.4% means 43/82 GRPO groups receive zero gradient (all G=8 rollouts fail → group mean = 0 → all advantages = 0). Sign advantage (EXP-108) addresses this at the *sequence level* (A=2r-1 gives A=−1 for failed rollouts). RLCSD addresses it at the *token level*: the contrastive term provides dense token-level gradient on every step, independent of whether any rollout passed. For code tasks, we adapt: correct_hint = first 48 tokens of GPT-5.5 correct trace (from traces.jsonl, SCALING_FORCE_BOTH=1); wrong_hint = first 48 tokens of the worst failing rollout. The contrastive difference cancels stylistic differences (indentation, variable naming) and concentrates on algorithmic decisions (loop structure, data structure, boundary conditions). Implementation: ~40 lines in `grpo_train_simple.py`, 2 extra forward passes per step (+~20% GPU time). Motivated **EXP-114**.

### 2. Evolutionary Strategies lead to Catastrophic Forgetting in LLMs ★★★
**ArXiv ID:** 2601.20861 | January 2026

**Core finding:** ES and GRPO reach comparable accuracy on math and reasoning tasks, but their gradient geometries are opposite: ES produces dense updates (broadly distributed, large ℓ2 norm) that cause significant catastrophic forgetting of prior capabilities; GRPO produces highly sparse updates (concentrated in a small parameter fraction) that cause much less forgetting. The paper attributes GRPO's lower forgetting to its update sparsity: sparse updates minimize interference with parameters encoding prior task knowledge.

**Relevance to us:** Our pipeline runs 4 GRPO cycles, each on traces from the previous cycle. Even with GRPO's inherently sparse updates, each cycle's gradient concentrates on *different task-subsets* (the tasks with non-zero advantage). A key unresolved question: does cycle-k GRPO (on hard tasks) interfere with parameters encoding easy tasks solved in cycle-(k-1)? If so, the Full=Router equivalence (92.68%) has two components: (1) ACR=52.4% (zero-variance groups, no gradient), and (2) cross-cycle forgetting (tasks regressed between cycles). EXP-108 (sign advantage) fixes component (1) but cannot fix component (2). Diagnosing the forgetting component first tells us whether EXC+EWC/replay is also needed for AAAI. Motivated **EXP-115**.

### 3. Improving LLM Code Generation via Requirement-Aware Curriculum RL ★★ (Reference)
**ArXiv ID:** 2605.00433 | May 1, 2026

**Core finding:** RECRL orders GRPO training tasks by difficulty (difficulty = 1 − pass_rate) across epochs: easy tasks first, hard tasks deferred. Achieves +1.23%–+5.62% Pass@1 over five baselines across 5 LLMs × 5 benchmarks. Key: training on hard tasks before the model is ready collapses gradients and destabilizes GRPO; easy tasks first provides positive gradient signal that warms up the policy.

**Relevance to today:** Already queued (EXP-110: RECRL standalone; EXP-113: RECRL + sign advantage composition). Noted today as *complementary to RLCSD* (EXP-114): RECRL reduces the *frequency* of zero-variance groups by ordering tasks; RLCSD provides token-level gradient for the *remaining* zero-variance groups. A three-way composition (RECRL + sign advantage + RLCSD) is a natural EXP-116 if EXP-113 and EXP-114 both show positive results.

### EXP-115: Forgetting Evaluation — Multi-Cycle Pass Rate Tracking (HumanEval)
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_13_002_forgetting_multicycle_gradient_sparsity_diagnostic`
**Kind:** `forgetting_eval` | **Priority:** 6 | **Estimated GPU:** 0.6h

**Motivation:** arxiv:2601.20861 (January 2026). GRPO updates are highly sparse vs ES, explaining lower forgetting per step. But our 4-cycle pipeline has never been analyzed for *cross-cycle forgetting within GRPO*. Does each cycle's gradient (concentrated on hard tasks with high advantage) interfere with parameters encoding easy tasks from prior cycles? If so, Full=Router has two components: (1) zero-variance groups (ACR=52.4%), and (2) cross-cycle forgetting. Sign advantage (EXP-108) fixes only component (1). This diagnostic distinguishes the two.

**Key differences from EXP-111 (RFT vs SFT cycle retention):**
- EXP-111: prescriptive — which method (RFT vs SFT) causes less forgetting?
- EXP-115: diagnostic — how much forgetting already occurs within the default pipeline (GRPO) and which tasks are affected?

**Implementation:** Eval-only, no training. Load 8 checkpoints (grpo_adapter or llm_adapter from each of 4 cycles). Run greedy pass@1 on all 82 HumanEval tasks per checkpoint. Build pass/fail matrix (4 cycles × 82 tasks). Compute: forgetting_flag per task, forgetting_rate overall and by difficulty bucket, Spearman correlation with per-task gradient norm from training logs. Total: ~0.6h.

**Expected outcomes:**
- **Low forgetting (<2%)**: ACR=52.4% is the dominant explanation; no replay needed. Sign advantage alone is the right fix.
- **Moderate forgetting (2–8%)**: forgetting contributes 1–6pp to Full=Router gap; EWC or replay buffer needed in addition to sign advantage (natural EXP-116).
- **High forgetting (>8%)**: novel finding — cross-cycle forgetting in iterative on-policy distillation; publishable as new §5.6 in AAAI paper.
