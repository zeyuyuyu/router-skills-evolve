## Top 5 Most Informative Results / Discoveries This Week

*(No new experiment completions; ranked by signal quality and actionability.)*

### 1. EGCA: +3.1pp HumanEval from execution-grounded credit assignment (EXP-054)
arxiv:2603.16158 (ICLR 2026 SPOT Workshop). EGCA localizes GRPO gradient to the earliest
statement-level execution divergence between the candidate and canonical solution, instead
of assigning uniform binary credit to every token. Published results: +3.1pp HumanEval,
+1.5pp MBPP vs vanilla GRPO, only 18% wall-clock overhead. This uses the existing MBPP
`canonical_solution` field — no new data. Of all 57 queued experiments, EGCA has the
strongest published prior evidence and the most principled mechanism: it directly addresses
the core flaw (gradient dilution from boilerplate tokens). **Priority 9, ~105 min.**

### 2. SA-AH-GRPO: +4.9pp and 3.6× variance reduction on 1.5B (EXP-055)
arxiv:2606.05434 (June 2026). Asymmetric entropy-adaptive horizon discounting: applies
cumulative entropy discount δ_t = Π exp(−β·H) only to negative-advantage (failing) rollouts,
leaving positive trajectories unattenuated. On Qwen2.5-1.5B LoRA fine-tuned on GSM8K:
+4.9pp over zero-shot, 3.6× lower training variance. The entropy discount is computed from
existing forward-pass logits — zero extra LLM calls. The variance reduction is particularly
valuable given our observed instability in the 1.5B training run. **Priority 7, ~95 min.**

### 3. Subspace geometry theory explains the 200→400 task cliff (EXP-053, EXP-056)
arxiv:2603.02224 (Feb 2026). Forgetting severity in LoRA sequential fine-tuning is governed
by the minimum principal angle (MPA) between consecutive task gradient subspaces. This
provides a rigorous theoretical explanation for our empirical observation: 200 tasks +2pp,
400 tasks −1pp. EXP-053 measures the MPA diagnostic (60 min). EXP-056 tests r=8 as a
zero-overhead mitigation (95 min). CapTrack (EXP-057) adds an explicit orthogonality penalty
as the prescriptive fix (115 min). Together these form a complete forgetting diagnosis → 
mitigation → verification chain.

### 4. Rollout waste quantified and addressed (EXP-050, EXP-051)
arxiv:2506.05316 + arxiv:2602.01601 together frame 73% of our G=4 rollout generation as
wasted compute (all-fail or all-pass groups). Difficulty-targeted selection + rollout replay
(EXP-050) achieves the same accuracy in 47% of wall-clock time on a 12B model. Adaptive
budget G∝√(p(1-p)) (EXP-051) concentrates the same 800-total rollouts on the 27% of tasks
that actually drive gradient. These are not just accuracy improvements — they materially
reduce the ~90–95 min training time per run, enabling faster iteration once A800 restores.

### 5. ReSkill directly targets the "skills-evolve" thesis (EXP-049)
arxiv:2606.01619 (June 1, 2026). ReSkill co-trains a persistent, task-agnostic skill library
alongside the GRPO policy, exploiting GRPO's group-wise rollout structure. Every 15 gradient
steps, the runner analyzes failing rollouts from mixed-outcome tasks, extracts recurring
failure patterns (boundary conditions, off-by-one errors), and uses the model itself to
generate conditional trigger-based skills injected into the system prompt. Within-group
comparison (G/2 with skills, G/2 without) validates each skill revision via gradient signal.
This is the first experiment directly testing the "skills-evolve" half of the project thesis
in the RL training loop. **Priority 8, ~100 min.**
