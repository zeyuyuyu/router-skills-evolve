## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. arxiv:2607.00152 — GRPO, Dr. GRPO, and DAPO Are Three Operations on One Number
**Bay & Yearick, June 30 2026**

**Core finding:** All three GRPO variants differ only in how they treat the within-group
standard deviation σ. For binary (pass/fail) rewards over K rollouts:

| Variant | Formula | Effective scale |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| GRPO    | `(r_i − mean_r) / σ` | `1/σ` — blows up when σ→0 |
| Dr. GRPO | `(r_i − mean_r)` | `p(1−p)` — naturally informative |
| DAPO    | GRPO but σ=0 groups filtered | never sees collapsed groups |

**Key identity:** For binary rewards, σ = √(p(1−p)·K/(K−1)), so Dr. GRPO's update
magnitude is exactly K/(K−1)·p(1−p)·‖∇log π‖ — largest at p=0.5 (maximally
informative prompt), smallest near p=0 or p=1 (trivially easy/hard). This is a
natural, parameter-free curriculum embedded in the gradient.

**Relevance:** Our ACR=52.4% means 43/82 GRPO groups are exactly collapsed (σ=0).
Standard GRPO's 1/σ is unstable there. Dr. GRPO gives zero gradient for perfectly
collapsed groups (correct) and stable p(1−p)-weighted gradients for all others.
Orthogonal to RAPER (EXP-118), MDP-GRPO (EXP-119), TACO (EXP-116).

### 2. arxiv:2507.00054 — Enhancing Reasoning Capabilities in SLMs with Reward Guided Dataset Distillation
**July 2026**

**Core finding:** SFT distillation from a large teacher is most effective in the
"Zone of Proximal Development" (ZPD): tasks where the student's own pass-rate
r_small ∈ [lo, hi]. Outside the ZPD:
- r_small > hi → student already solves the task → wasted training signal
- r_small < lo → task is beyond the student's reach → distribution mismatch risk

**Method:** Score each teacher trace by the student's empirical pass-rate on that
task (from rollout data). Retain only traces in the ZPD band. Reported improvement:
**+3.1–5.6pp** over naive SFT on math and code benchmarks at identical example counts.

**Relevance:** Directly targets our Root Problem (C): the skills gap (75.61% vs
96.34%, a 20.7pp deficit). `collect_traces.py` already records per-prompt small-model
rewards in `traces.jsonl`; the filter is a 2-line addition to `traces_to_sft.py`.
No changes to GRPO or router. Orthogonal to all ACR-collapse fixes.

### 3. arxiv:2603.18079 — SLEA-RL: Step-Level Experience Augmented RL for Multi-Turn Agentic Training
**March 2026 (surfaced in July sweeps)**

**Core finding:** Combines episode-level GRPO advantage with step-level credit
assignment: group steps with similar observations across trajectories, then
normalize discounted returns within each step-observation group. For multi-turn
coding tasks (debug loops), this gives higher credit to the pivotal diagnosis
step vs. mechanical editing steps.

**Relevance:** Currently our GRPO uses episode-level binary rewards (pass/fail the
full test suite). In multi-turn repair trajectories, the diagnosis step is the
bottleneck — uniform credit is wasteful. SLEA-RL's step-level groups would
concentrate gradient on the fix-attempt step. Candidate for a future experiment
once A800 is restored and multi-turn agentic coding is enabled.

### EXP-120: Dr. GRPO (no std normalization) + sign-advantage fallback
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_17_001_dr_grpo_no_std_sign_adv_fallback_humaneval`  
**Priority:** 8 · **Kind:** `grpo_continual` · **GPU hours:** ~2.5h

**Addresses:** Root problem (A) — ACR=52.4% / Full arm = Router  
**Arxiv:** 2607.00152  

**What changes:**
- Advantage normalization: remove the `/ (std_r + eps)` denominator
- Advantages become `r_i − mean_r` (unnormalized)
- Zero-variance fallback: for collapsed groups (std=0), apply sign-advantage
  (all-fail → A = −1; all-pass → A = 0, no gradient)
- Everything else unchanged (T=1.0, K=8, same LoRA config)

**Why now:** RAPER (EXP-118) and MDP-GRPO (EXP-119) were queued yesterday.
Dr. GRPO is theoretically the purest solution — it directly implements the
mathematical insight that the std division is what causes instability at
σ→0, and replacing it with no-division gives p(1−p)-weighted gradients that
act as a built-in curriculum. It takes only ~10 lines of code vs RAPER's ~50 lines.

**Expected outcome:** ACR drops below 45% (natural: perfectly collapsed groups
get zero gradient rather than amplified noise); full arm pass@1 ≥ 93.0%.
