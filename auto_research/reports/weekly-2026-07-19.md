## Headline Findings (Top 5 Most Informative This Week)

Since no A800 experiments ran, "results" are high-fidelity arxiv signals directly relevant
to our 52.4% ACR bottleneck and 20.7pp skills gap. Ranked by signal-to-noise:

### 1. AVSPO (arxiv:2605.21125, ICML 2026) — ACR Metric + 4–6pp on Code/Math
**Why informative:** Independently formalizes exactly the metric (ACR = Advantage Collapse
Rate) that we empirically identified at 52.4%. ICML acceptance provides peer-reviewed
validation of the bottleneck. The synthetic virtual-sample injection achieves **ACR
−58–63% relative** and **+4–6pp code/math** across 0.5B–14B models. This is the strongest
external validation of our primary bottleneck and the best-documented remedy (code released).
→ Queued as EXP-122.

### 2. Group-SD Identity (arxiv:2607.00152) — Theoretical Unification of GRPO Variants
**Why informative:** Proves all three GRPO variants (GRPO, Dr. GRPO, DAPO) differ only in
treatment of within-group σ. For binary rewards: σ = √(p(1−p)·K/(K−1)). Dr. GRPO's
update magnitude = K/(K−1)·p(1−p)·‖∇log π‖ — a natural parameter-free curriculum
(gradient ∝ difficulty signal, not σ-amplified noise). This directly converts our 52.4%
σ=0 finding into a theoretical prediction: Dr. GRPO gives **zero gradient** for perfectly
collapsed groups (provably correct, not NaN-clipped) while giving stable gradients for all
others. Added to paper v6 abstract; Dr. GRPO queued as EXP-120.

### 3. CPO (arxiv:2607.04364) — RL Forgets! Challenges "RFT Natural Mitigation" Claim
**Why informative:** arxiv:2507.05386 (cited in our paper) claimed RFT "naturally" mitigates
forgetting. CPO directly refutes this under diverse sequential tasks: RL fine-tuning does
forget significantly when task distributions diverge between cycles. **This reframes our
tau2 oscillation (89.19%→70.27%→72.97%)**: previously attributed to SFT-specific forgetting,
now may have a GRPO component too. CPO's replay-free behavioral KL regularization is
LoRA-compatible (~60 lines). Queued as EXP-117 (conditional) and EXP-125 (independent).

### 4. ZPD-SFT (arxiv:2507.00054) — +3.1–5.6pp via Zone of Proximal Development Filter
**Why informative:** Our skills gap is 20.7pp (75.61% vs. 96.34%). ZPD-SFT shows that
filtering teacher traces to the student's ZPD (pass-rate ∈ [0.2, 0.8]) yields **+3.1–5.6pp
at matched example counts** on math and code benchmarks. Implementation is a **2-line change
to traces_to_sft.py** (our `collect_traces.py` already records per-prompt small-model
rewards). Zero GPU cost, pure data-efficiency. Highest ratio of predicted gain to
implementation cost in the current queue. Queued as EXP-121.

### 5. TACO (arxiv:2607.07976) — Positive-Credit Contamination: +0.8–1.5pp Code, +1.3–2.8pp Math
**Why informative:** Identifies a failure mode within non-collapsed groups (the 47.6% that
DO provide gradient): winning rollouts receive equal positive credit for all tokens, including
low-probability "tail tokens" that are stylistically coincidental rather than algorithmically
critical. TACO's credit suppression is a **~15-line modification** orthogonal to all other
ACR fixes (targets only non-collapsed groups, which are the exclusive source of learning).
+0.8–1.5pp code improvement is modest but consistent across models. Queued as EXP-116.
