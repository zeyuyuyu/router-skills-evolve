## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. EP-GRPO: Entropy-Progress Aligned GRPO with Implicit Process Guidance
**arxiv:2605.04960** | Song Yu, Li Li, Wenwen Zhao, Zhisheng Yang | May 6, 2026

EP-GRPO addresses three credit assignment failures in standard GRPO: (1) uniform token-level granularity ignoring heterogeneous informational value, (2) uniform polarity that penalizes correct steps/rewards incorrect ones, and (3) zero-variance collapse (identical to our ACR problem). It adds three modular terms to the GRPO objective:
- **Entropy-gated modulation**: amplify gradients at tokens where policy entropy exceeds reference entropy (decision pivots)
- **Implicit process signals**: KL divergence from reference policy, anchored to outcome advantages, for directional token-level feedback without any external reward model
- **Cumulative entropy mapping**: progress-aligned advantage normalization replacing flat mean-subtraction in collapsed groups

**Relevance:** The implicit process signal creates within-group gradient differentiation from intermediate generation dynamics — even when all K rollouts have identical final rewards (R=0 or R=1). This directly attacks our ACR=52.4% bottleneck. No queued experiment uses this mechanism. Motivated **EXP-102**.

### 2. DARS: Distribution-Aware Routing Supervision
**arxiv:2606.06924** | Guannan Lai, Haoran Hu, Long Chen, Zhenguo Li, Han-Jia Ye | Nanjing U / HKUST | June 2026

DARS proposes replacing single-shot binary routing labels with distributional capability estimates. Key finding: single-shot LLM outputs are noisy; routing supervision should use the empirical pass rate across K sampled rollouts (p̂_K) as soft labels, trained with Brier score loss instead of binary cross-entropy. DARS also considers semantic input variation, but the output-side distributional signal alone improves routing across diverse tasks.

**Relevance:** We already collect K=8 GRPO rollouts per task from the small model (SCALING_FORCE_BOTH=1). The p̂_8 soft label is available at zero extra compute. Our binary router label conflates borderline tasks (p̂_8=0.375, small model sometimes solves) with hard tasks (p̂_8=0), causing router miscalibration. DARS supervision could push routing accuracy above 92.68%. Motivated **EXP-103**.

### 3. LLM Evolution as an Industry-Scale Ecosystem: A Lifecycle Perspective on Continual Learning
**arxiv:2606.24901** | June 2026

Analyzes continual learning for LLMs from a lifecycle perspective across production deployment. Identifies that naive iterative fine-tuning leads to capability drift as cycles compound, and proposes lifecycle-aware training strategies. The key insight: later cycles should explicitly preserve earlier task knowledge rather than implicitly relying on regularization.

**Relevance:** Our 4-cycle pipeline shows Full=Router at cycle 3 (GRPO stagnates). This paper suggests that by cycle 3, the SFT→GRPO training has compounded enough cycle-over-cycle drift to degrade GRPO's effectiveness. Supports the already-queued forgetting evaluation experiments (EXP-076, EXP-087) and provides theoretical grounding for CRAFT-style gradient projection approaches. Noted as background literature for the AAAI paper's related work section.

### EXP-102: EP-GRPO Implicit Process Guidance for ACR Reduction (HumanEval)
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_02_001_epgrpo_process_guidance_acr_humaneval`
**Kind:** `grpo_continual` | **Priority:** 7 | **Estimated GPU:** 1.75h

**Motivation:** arxiv:2605.04960. All 10+ queued ACR mitigations modify reward functions, gradient weights, or inject virtual samples. EP-GRPO is the only approach that creates within-group gradient differentiation from the model's own intermediate policy divergence — without changing rewards or adding samples. The implicit process signal (KL divergence from reference, anchored to outcome advantages) activates even in all-fail groups where binary reward provides zero gradient.

**Spec:** 1-cycle DAPO+EP-GRPO on HumanEval, Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B, G=8, SCALING_FORCE_BOTH=1. Adds three loss terms: entropy-gated modulation (λ_gate), implicit process signal (λ_proc=0.1), cumulative entropy normalization (λ_ent=0.05). Compares against binary DAPO baseline (ACR=52.4%).

**Target:** ACR ≤ 40% (from 52.4%), HumanEval pass@1 ≥ 83%. Entropy-gate activations confirm process signal is non-trivial in collapsed groups.

### EXP-103: DARS Distribution-Aware Router Supervision (HumanEval, zero extra compute)
**ID:** `exp_2026_07_02_002_dars_distribution_router_softlabel_humaneval`
**Kind:** `forgetting_eval` | **Priority:** 6 | **Estimated GPU:** 1.0h (mostly CPU)

**Motivation:** arxiv:2606.06924. Our logistic router trains on binary oracle labels (does small model solve it?), which are noisy single-shot observations. SCALING_FORCE_BOTH=1 already provides K=8 rollouts per task — the empirical pass rate p̂_8 is a free DARS-style soft label. Borderline tasks (p̂_8 ∈ {2/8, 3/8, 4/8}) are currently mislabeled as either always-route-small (label=0) or always-route-large (label=1). DARS supervision trains the router to predict p̂_8, then sweeps the routing threshold on validation to optimize cost-accuracy tradeoff.

**Spec:** Load existing traces from e2e_4cyc_gpt55/cycle_3. Compute p̂_8 from `small_rollout_results` field. Retrain router with Brier score loss and soft labels. Threshold sweep on validation. Compare routing accuracy and Brier calibration vs. binary-label baseline (92.68%).

**Target:** Routing accuracy ≥ 93.5%, improved Brier score calibration. Borderline tasks routed more accurately. Potential AAAI Table 2 appendix entry: "DARS vs. binary-label router ablation."
