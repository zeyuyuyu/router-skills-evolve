## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. "DAPO: An Open-Source LLM Reinforcement Learning System at Scale" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2503.14476 | March 2025 | DeepSeek (Qiao et al.)

**Core finding**: DAPO extends GRPO with dynamic sampling (resample prompts where all rollouts produce identical rewards) and a clip_higher objective. Section 4.2 provides the key theoretical analysis: binary pass/fail rewards create two structurally zero-advantage regimes — all-pass groups (trivially easy) and all-fail groups (too hard) — neither of which contributes any gradient. Dynamic sampling partially mitigates this by resampling prompts, but at cycle 3 we observe 52.4% ACR persisting even with dynamic sampling active.

**Why relevant today**: The reward function design is the upstream cause of ACR. All queued ACR-mitigation experiments (EXP-090, EXP-094, EXP-088) modify training dynamics after the reward is assigned — they cannot create within-group variance from a group where all rewards are literally identical (all pass: R=1 for every rollout). Adding a continuous secondary component (length penalty) to the binary reward directly addresses the DAPO §4.2 failure mode at the source, before any of the downstream modifications apply.

**Motivation for EXP-096**: Length-penalty reward supplement reduces ACR by creating nonzero within-group variance even in all-pass groups, because rollout lengths vary (σ_len ≈ 15-30 tokens) while binary outcomes do not. The penalty is calibrated (α=0.1, L_max=150 tokens) to preserve the pass/fail signal dominance while adding differentiating variance.

### 2. "Rethinking Predictive Modeling for LLM Routing: When Simple kNN Beats Complex Learned Routers" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2505.12601 | May 2025

**Core finding**: Across multiple routing benchmarks, a k-Nearest Neighbor router using cosine similarity on pre-trained sentence embeddings matches or exceeds the routing accuracy of trained classifiers (logistic regression, gradient-boosted trees, shallow MLPs). The paper argues that routing difficulty is dominated by query-type proximity — code vs. math vs. knowledge tasks — and that this is captured as well by embedding-space nearestneighbors as by a trained model. Complex learned classifiers add training overhead without accuracy gains in the typical routing regime.

**Why relevant now**: This was explicitly flagged as "deferred post-EMNLP" in ideas-2026-06-17.md. EMNLP deadline was 2026-06-19 (10 days ago). With AAAI 2027 deadline on 2026-08-15 (47 days), the kNN ablation is now the highest-priority unanswered architectural question for the MERA paper. If kNN matches our 92.68% trained router, the paper's router section simplifies dramatically. If the trained router outperforms kNN, that validates Phase 4 training as essential — a stronger claim for AAAI.

**Motivation for EXP-097**: Run kNN routing on cycle-3 traces from both HumanEval and tau2 using two encoder variants (general sentence-BERT + cycle-3 SFT small model as encoder). CPU-only, ~15 minutes. Results directly answer the AAAI §5 router ablation.
