## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. "Infinite Sampling: Efficient and Stable Grouped RL Training for Large Language Models" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2506.22950 | June 2025

**Core finding**: GRPO's group size G is constrained by GPU memory — every additional
rollout requires holding another completion in GPU VRAM during advantage computation.
At G=4 (our current setting), zero-variance collapse (all rollouts same outcome) is
the dominant gradient-signal pathology: at mid-training pass_rate ≈ 0.7, P(all-pass
in G=4) ≈ 24%; at late-training pass_rate ≈ 0.9, P(all-pass) ≈ 66%. This explains
why training improvement stalls despite the model still making errors on harder prompts
— the prompts the model is "mostly getting" produce near-zero gradient, while the
prompts the model consistently fails produce all-fail groups that also yield zero
advantage.

**Infinite Sampling** breaks the memory-G coupling via:
1. **Micro-sampling groups**: split the logical group G=N into rounds of g samples each,
   running each round sequentially (generation + gradient accumulation).
2. **Continuous sampling**: pipeline round k+1's generation with round k's training step,
   keeping the GPU busy and recovering most of the sequential overhead.
3. **Full-group advantage normalization**: advantages are computed across all N samples
   from all rounds, giving a lower-variance advantage estimator than per-round G=g.

**Results**: G=16 achieves improvements over G=4 comparable to simply having 4× the GPU
count, without requiring multi-GPU setup. The pipeline overlap keeps wall-clock at
~1.5-1.8× baseline for G=8, and ~2.5-3.0× for G=16.

**Why actioned today as EXP-072 (G=8)**:
- At G=8: P(all-pass at 0.7) = 0.7^8 ≈ 6% (vs. 24% at G=4 — **4× reduction**)
- At G=8: P(all-pass at 0.9) = 0.9^8 ≈ 43% (vs. 66% at G=4 — **35% reduction**)
- No change to reward, model, task count, LoRA, or training schedule
- Mechanistically complementary to EP-GRPO (EXP-068): EP-GRPO compensates for
  zero-variance groups after they form via process signal injection; Infinite Sampling
  prevents them from forming in the first place
- Wall-clock ~110 min (within 4-hour A800 budget; Infinite Sampling pipeline overlap)

**Distinct from existing queue**: No queued experiment has `rollouts_per_prompt > 4`
or `micro_sampling=True`. The distinction from EP-GRPO is structural (G size vs.
token-level entropy gating). ✓

### 2. "Robust Policy Optimization to Prevent Catastrophic Forgetting" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2602.08813 | February 2026 | GitHub: Helloworld10011/FRPO

**Core finding**: Standard RLHF/GRPO maximizes reward at the current policy θ but is
oblivious to downstream drift: when subsequent fine-tuning steps (on new tasks) shift
θ to θ', performance on earlier tasks (E_{θ'}[R_old]) can collapse. The brittleness
comes from the fact that standard objectives create sharp, high-reward peaks in policy
space — small movements off the peak cause large reward drops.

**FRPO (Fine-tuning Robust Policy Optimization)** modifies the objective to:

```
max_θ  min_{KL(π', π) ≤ ε}  E_{π'}[R]
```

i.e., find the policy that maximizes expected reward even for the *worst-case policy
within a KL ball* around it. This creates a **flat reward landscape** in the KL
neighborhood of θ, so that subsequent fine-tuning steps that move θ to θ' within
the KL ball cause only small reward degradation.

**Implementation**: FRPO adds a minimax regularization term to the GRPO loss. The
inner minimization is solved via Lagrangian relaxation (dual variable update),
adding ~5-8% compute overhead per step. Code is available at the linked GitHub.

**Why actioned today as EXP-073 at 400 tasks**:
- The 400-task degradation (47→46, -1pp) is the principal unexplained failure in our
  history. Three mechanistic hypotheses exist: (A) entropy collapse — being tested by
  EXP-070 (Entrocraft) and EXP-071 (GTPO); (B) zero-variance groups — being tested by
  EXP-068 (EP-GRPO) and EXP-072 (Infinite Sampling G=8); (C) catastrophic forgetting
  of early-task representations due to sequential policy drift.
- FRPO directly tests hypothesis (C): if sequential policy drift is the cause, FRPO's
  robust objective should stabilize the drift and allow 400-task training to achieve
  ≥47 (matching or exceeding 200-task baseline), not 46.
- Testing at 400 tasks is critical — at 200 tasks GRPO improves, so the forgetting
  mechanism is only observable at higher task counts.
- kl_neighborhood_radius=0.1: chosen to match the paper's recommended default for
  code-domain downstream adaptation scenarios (vs. 0.05 for math reasoning in paper).

**Distinct from existing queue**:
- No queued experiment uses `loss_type="frpo"` or `kl_neighborhood_radius > 0`
- EXP-071 (GTPO) removes reference model entirely (kl_coeff=0) — a different direction
- MoFO (arxiv:2407.20999, deferred) modifies the optimizer momentum to protect
  previously learned weights — orthogonal approach (optimizer-level vs. objective-level)
- EXP-053 (forgetting_eval) measures forgetting — it does not attempt to prevent it ✓

### 3. "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning Naturally Mitigates Forgetting in Continual Post-Training" ★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2507.05386 | July 2025

**Core finding**: Side-by-side comparison of SFT and GRPO-based RFT on continual
post-training. SFT leads to catastrophic forgetting of prior tasks; GRPO-based RFT
preserves prior knowledge substantially better. The mechanism: RFT reinforces correct
samples that are *already aligned with the base model's probability landscape*, so
gradient updates interfere less with existing representations.

**Why relevant but not actioned today**: We are already using GRPO (RFT), so this
paper confirms our algorithmic choice rather than suggesting a new direction. The
finding also provides a theoretical lower bound: if GRPO is supposed to mitigate
forgetting naturally, but our 400-task run still degrades, then something beyond the
base mechanism is at play (supporting the FRPO hypothesis in EXP-073). The paper
uses Qwen2.5-VL-7B (multimodal), but the mechanism is domain-general.

**Why deferred**: No immediately actionable variant to test beyond our existing GRPO
baseline. EXP-073 (FRPO) is a higher-leverage intervention. If FRPO fails, this
paper motivates an investigation into whether our training data construction (binary
reward signal with no reasoning trace) reduces the degree to which RFT's "natural
forgetting mitigation" takes effect — potentially motivating a CoT scratchpad
experiment (see 2506.23508).
