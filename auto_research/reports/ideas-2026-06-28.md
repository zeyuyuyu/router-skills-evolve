## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. Advantage Collapse in Group Relative Policy Optimization: Diagnosis and Mitigation
**arxiv:2605.21125** · May 21, 2026

**What it does:** Introduces the Advantage Collapse Rate (ACR) — the fraction of training
batches where within-group reward variance is below a threshold, yielding vanishing GRPO
gradients. Across 0.5B–14B models on math reasoning, ACR strongly predicts training
stagnation and final accuracy. The mitigation is AVSPO (Adaptive Virtual Sample Policy
Optimization): when a group's ACR is triggered, inject K_virt virtual rollouts with
synthetically interpolated rewards r_virt ~ Uniform(r_min - δ, r_max + δ). The injection
rate K_virt is gated by an exponential moving average of recent ACR, so virtual samples
are only added when real diversity is absent. Reports 58–63% ACR reduction and 4–6 pp
accuracy gains vs. vanilla GRPO across all model scales.

**Why relevant:** We have directly measured ACR = 52.4% on HumanEval GRPO at cycle 3
(43/82 task groups per batch have zero within-group reward variance). This is precisely
the failure mode AVSPO targets. Our EXP-090 (STARE, queued) works at the token-reweighting
level and cannot help when ALL rollouts in a group have the same reward (zero advantage).
AVSPO and STARE are complementary: AVSPO restores group-level advantage diversity;
STARE then refines the within-group token-level distribution. ACR = 52.4% maps directly
to K_virt ≈ 2 (K_virt_max=4 × 0.524 ≈ 2.1), giving a concrete starting configuration.

**Distinctness:** Not cited in any prior queue update. Targets group-sampling level vs.
STARE (token level) and G2PO (multi-turn graph). ✓

### 2. Drowning in Routine: Signal Dilution in Multi-Turn Agent Training
**arxiv:2606.22164** · June 27, 2026 (FAGEN Workshop @ ICML 2026)

**What it does:** Formalizes a new failure mode in multi-turn agent RL: signal dilution
caused by low decision density ρ. Decision density ρ = fraction of turns whose action
actually affects P(return). When many turns are "routine" (reward-equivalent), they add
gradient variance without adding expected gradient signal. The key theoretical result:
trajectory-level estimators like GRPO suffer SNR ∝ ρ^{1/2} — halving ρ degrades SNR by
29%; reducing ρ 10× degrades SNR by 68%. Validated on the "Diluted Doors" MDP where ρ
is exactly tunable: the ρ^{-1/2} law explains 99.9% of empirical SNR variation across a
50-fold ρ range. Prescription: identify high-ρ turns via Var_{rollouts}[R | turn t] and
up-weight their gradients by 1/sqrt(ρ_t).

**Why relevant:** Our tau2 multi-turn repair setting has exactly the structure that makes
signal dilution severe. Turn-0 (write candidate solution) contributes high diversity.
Turn-1 (repair from test failure) is the decision-dense turn — specific repair reasoning
strongly determines final pass/fail. Turn-2 (minor formatting/import fix, reached by ~30%
of tasks) is often routine: given the turn-1 state, most reasonable turn-2 actions either
all pass or all fail. This turn-2 routineness dilutes gradient from the high-ρ turn-1
repair reasoning. GRPO treats all turns symmetrically, so routine turns wash out signal.
EXP-091 (TRUST) weights by entropy, EXP-092 (G2PO) uses graph credit — both are
orthogonal to the decision-density criterion, which weighs by causal impact on the return.

**Distinctness:** arxiv:2606.22164 was posted June 27, 2026 — the day before this run.
Not cited in any prior queue update. The decision-density-weighted advantage (DDWA)
approach is structurally distinct from all queued EXPs. ✓

### 3. SoftMoE: Soft Differentiable Routing for Mixture-of-Experts in LLMs
**arxiv:2606.17952** · June 16, 2026 (ICML 2026)

**What it does:** Replaces discrete top-k expert routing in MoE LLMs with a truncated
soft top-k LapSum relaxation that is fully differentiable. Further parameterizes the mean
number of active experts per layer and imposes a global FLOP budget constraint, allowing
the model to learn to allocate compute adaptively across layers. Achieves parity or better
vs. sparse MoE on language modeling while activating significantly fewer experts.

**Why relevant (indirect):** Our task-level router (logistic regression on raw prompts)
makes hard binary routing decisions at inference time. SoftMoE's contribution is showing
that differentiable routing — where gradient flows through the routing decision during
training — can improve routing quality by allowing the routing signal to backpropagate
into the representation from which routing decisions are made. Applying this concept to
our setting: during SFT, use a soft (temperature-annealed) routing gate instead of a hard
binary label, so SFT representation learning is influenced by routing uncertainty.

**Potential EXP (not queued today due to queue cap):** Differentiable soft router trained
jointly with the small model's SFT objective: use a soft routing gate σ(logit/τ) with
τ annealed from 2.0 → 0.5 across cycles. The routing logit is computed from the small
model's [CLS]-equivalent embedding, so SFT gradients flow through the routing gate and
shape the representation to be more router-friendly. This joint training would resolve
the current decoupling (router trained separately post-SFT on frozen embeddings).
Saved for a future day when queue pressure decreases. (arxiv:2606.17952, ICML 2026)

### EXP-095 — Drowning-in-Routine Decision Density Weighted Advantage for tau2
**ID:** `exp_2026_06_28_002_drowning_routine_decision_density_tau2`
**Priority:** 7
**Kind:** `grpo_continual`
**Bench:** tau2
**Linked paper:** arxiv:2606.22164

**Rationale summary:** tau2's multi-turn repair setting has a known signal dilution
problem: turn-2 (reached by ~30% of tasks) is often routine relative to turn-1's repair
reasoning. Pernot & Retault's ρ^{-1/2} SNR law quantifies the gradient degradation from
routine turns. DDWA (Decision Density Weighted Advantage) scales each turn's gradient
contribution by 1/sqrt(ρ̂_t), approximated via a positional proxy (turn-1 gets 2× weight
of turn-2). This concentrates gradient on consequential repair decisions and down-weights
boilerplate turns. Orthogonal to G2PO (graph credit) and TRUST (entropy weighting) —
all three can eventually be composed.

**Target:** tau2 always-small pass@1 ≥ 79% after 2 cycles (from 76.97% baseline);
empirical validation that ρ̂_turn1 > ρ̂_turn0 > ρ̂_turn2.
