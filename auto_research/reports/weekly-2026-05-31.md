## Top 5 Informative Results

### 1. Tau2-4B-273: 42% step imitation, 0% autonomy  
`05_qwen3_5_4b_273` step-budget eval (primary seed, 385 rollouts, $20.32):

| Method | Pass Rate |
|---|---:|
| Method B (step imitation) | **42.0%** (148/352) |
| Method A (autonomous E2E) | **0.0%** (0/33) |

Domain breakdown: Airline 43.0%, Retail 39.0%, Telecom 32.2%.  
**Signal:** SFT on trajectory data teaches local step imitation but fails completely at
end-to-end autonomous completion. The 42-point Method A gap is the sharpest new finding
of the week.

### 2. SFT loss curves confirm effective training  
All 4B (eval-loss 0.1780→0.1711) and 9B (0.1561) checkpoints show training loss descent
(4B-273: 0.1099→0.0724). The model is fitting the distribution; the problem is generalization
to compounding errors in Method A, not optimization failure.

### 3. Method A failure anatomy  
With 0/33 Method A passes, the failure is total, not marginal. This rules out
"almost working" and points to a distribution mismatch between teacher-forced (Method B)
and compounding-error (Method A) regimes. RL-based training on complete trajectories is
the natural remedy.

### 4. POPO positive-only RL (arxiv:2605.06650) — new algorithm for the GRPO ceiling  
POPO trains exclusively on rollout groups that have ≥1 passing rollout, using IS-weighted
implicit negatives. For our MBPP setup: at each gradient step only groups with ≥1 pass
provide gradient; all-fail groups (53% of training tasks) are dynamically skipped without
any pre-exploration overhead. Unlike DAPO (explicit mixed-outcome requirement) or EXP-039
(static pre-filter), POPO uses the current policy's natural positive-rollout distribution
and self-heals as training improves. This is the most elegant new approach to the
73%-silent-group problem yet queued. (EXP-040 queued, ~85 min on A800 restoration.)
