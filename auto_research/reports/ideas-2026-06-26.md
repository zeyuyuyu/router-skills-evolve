## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. STARE: Surprisal-Guided Token-Level Advantage Reweighting for Policy Entropy Stability
**arxiv:2606.19236** · Luo et al. (Tsinghua University & Tencent Hunyuan) · June 17, 2026

**What it does:** Conducts a first-order gradient analysis of token-level entropy
dynamics under GRPO and identifies an **advantage-surprisal four-quadrant structure**:
the per-token entropy change decomposes as dH_t = A_r × f(S_t), where S_t = -log
π_ref(a_t|s_t) is the token's surprisal under the reference policy and f(·) is an
entropy sensitivity function. This yields four regimes:
- (+advantage, +surprisal) → entropy explosion on creative tokens (helpful)
- (+advantage, −surprisal) → boilerplate tokens reinforced as strongly as the creative ones (wasteful)
- (−advantage, +surprisal) → high-surprisal tokens in failing rollouts suffer entropy collapse (harmful)
- (−advantage, −surprisal) → benign, low-signal correction

STARE's solution: weight each token's policy gradient by its normalized batch-internal
surprisal `w_t = S_t / mean_t(S_t)`. This concentrates gradient on the tokens where
the policy actually diverges from reference (the "creative" steps), while down-weighting
boilerplate. The result is entropy near-criticality (dH/dt ≈ 0 throughout training)
without an auxiliary entropy loss term.

**Why relevant:** Our 52.4% zero-variance group discard rate is the top GRPO bottleneck
(EXP-088/VIMPO targets zero-variance elimination; STARE targets entropy collapse in the
remaining 47.6% non-zero-variance groups). STARE is complementary: VIMPO addresses
all-same-outcome groups; STARE addresses token-level credit misattribution within the
groups that DO have variance. The two can be composed (STARE + VIMPO) in a future run.

**Implementation cost:** ~15 lines in `grpo_train_simple.py`; uses already-computed
reference logprobs (present in KL-penalty computation). Surprisal S_t = -ref_logp.

**Distinctness:** Never mentioned in any prior report or pending queue update. ✓
EXP-088 (VIMPO) uses suffix log-ratio sums; EXP-055 (SA-AH-GRPO) uses entropy-adaptive
horizon discounting; EXP-082 (RL-ZVP) penalizes zero-variance post-hoc. STARE's
surprisal-weighted advantage is a different mechanism from all three.

### 2. TRUST: Exploring Agentic Tool-Calling Decisions via Uncertainty-Aligned Reinforcement Learning
**arxiv:2606.06976** · June 2026

**What it does:** Identifies that multi-turn agent trajectories suffer from **credit
assignment dilution**: all turns in a trajectory receive the same outcome-level reward,
even though some turns are high-uncertainty decision points (which tool to call next,
whether to retry a failed operation) while others are low-uncertainty boilerplate
(filling in required parameters, syntactic completion). TRUST addresses this with:
1. **Turn-level uncertainty weighting**: For each turn k, compute H_k = average policy
   entropy over that turn's tokens. Weight the turn's gradient contribution by
   softmax(H_k) across turns — high-entropy (decision) turns get more gradient.
2. **Uncertainty-separation regularizer**: For pairs (k, k') where H_k > H_{k'}, add a
   KL-divergence term that encourages the policy to maintain distinct action distributions
   at decision turns vs. boilerplate turns (λ_sep = 0.01).

Result: on a 4-turn tool-calling benchmark, TRUST reduces hallucinated tool invocations
by 37% and unsupported direct responses by 29% vs. standard multi-turn GRPO.

**Why relevant:** Our HumanEval Phase 3b uses up to 3-turn repair rollouts. Standard
DAPO applies a uniform outcome reward across all turns. For the ~30% of problems that
require multi-turn repair, TRUST's per-turn entropy weighting would upweight turn-2
(the critical "repair strategy" decision) relative to turn-1 boilerplate structure.
This directly addresses credit dilution in our existing repair setup without requiring
additional infrastructure (turn boundaries already tracked via stop tokens).

**Distinctness:** Never mentioned in any prior report or pending queue update. ✓
EXP-031 (MURPHY) uses execution-trace divergence for retrospective credit; TRUST uses
per-turn policy entropy — distinct signal sources. EXP-089 (UCCI) changes router
calibration post-training, not GRPO reward during training.
