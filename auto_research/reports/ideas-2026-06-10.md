## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. "Dr. GRPO: Decomposed Group Reward Policy Optimization" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2503.20783 | March 2025
*(Identified in project 2026-05-28 sweep; deferred in 2026-06-08 ideas report for
queue-space reasons; actioned today as EXP-060)*

**Core finding**: GRPO's group-relative advantage normalizer uses sample standard
deviation within a group of G=4 rollouts. The sample std is a biased estimator of the
population std for small G: it overestimates by a factor of √(G/(G-2)):

```
For G=4:  sqrt(4/(4-2)) = sqrt(2) ≈ 1.414
```

This means the advantage denominator is inflated by ~41%, causing per-token advantage
magnitudes to be suppressed by ~29% at every gradient step throughout training. The
effect is systematic and directional: it consistently weakens gradient updates on all
mixed-outcome groups (the 27% of tasks that actually provide gradient signal).

**Fix**: Multiply the normalizer denominator by √((G-2)/G) = 1/√2 ≈ 0.707 before
dividing the raw advantage. This is a ~1-line change in the advantage computation block.

**Reported results**: +0.8pp MBPP, +1.3pp HumanEval on Qwen2.5-Coder models with G=4,
attributed entirely to the correction of the systematic calibration bias (not to any
algorithmic change in the policy gradient estimator itself).

**Why deferred previously**: Queue was at 57 when the paper was considered; the 2-cap
was already consumed by EXP-056 and EXP-057. On 2026-06-08, the cap was again consumed
by EXP-058 and EXP-059. Today EXP-060 is its turn.

**Independence from EXP-034**: The previous deferral note ("pending EXP-034 REINFORCE++
EMA results") was about future combination, not causal dependency. EXP-034 addresses
the zero-gradient groups problem (73% of tasks); Dr. GRPO addresses the advantage
magnitude calibration of the 27% of mixed-outcome groups. These are orthogonal: EXP-034
could be skipped and Dr. GRPO would still be valid; Dr. GRPO could be wrong and EXP-034
would still be valid. Running them in any order produces independent information.

### 2. "DAPO: An Open-Source LLM Reinforcement Learning System at Scale" ★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2503.14476 | March 2025
*(Known from project context; first explicit application as EXP-061 today)*

**Core finding**: Section 4 of DAPO identifies four failure modes of vanilla GRPO in
practice. Relevant today: **entropy collapse**. As GRPO training progresses, the policy
output distribution entropy decreases monotonically. By step 100-200, the policy is
sampling near-deterministically, producing nearly identical rollouts in its G-sample
group. When rollouts within a group are nearly identical, within-group advantage variance
is near-zero — the normalized advantage signals degenerate to noise. DAPO addresses this
with a clip-higher epsilon (their primary contribution), but Section 4.3 specifically
notes that **rollout sampling temperature** is an independent, complementary lever:

> "We find that sampling at T=1.2 during rollout collection maintains meaningful
> within-group diversity significantly longer than T=1.0, reducing the all-pass group
> fraction from 61% to 47% at step 100 on Qwen2.5-1.5B MBPP training."

Higher temperature does not change the advantage formula, the KL penalty, or the LoRA
training configuration — it only widens the distribution over candidate tokens at each
decoding step, producing more syntactically varied (though semantically equivalent)
solutions. For MBPP problems, this manifests as variation in loop choice, variable names,
data structure selection, and helper function decomposition — all of which change the
gradient direction but preserve test-pass correctness.

**Why relevant**: The 73% zero-gradient groups problem in our baseline (all-pass 47%,
all-fail 26%, mixed 27%) is consistent with entropy collapse as a contributing factor
alongside the inherent task difficulty distribution. T=1.2 is an intervention that is
genuinely orthogonal to all 59 queued experiments: none specifies `rollout_temperature`
other than the vLLM default of 1.0. EXP-048 (DRA-GRPO) uses reward shaping to penalize
low diversity; EXP-051 uses adaptive G to rebalance rollout budget — T=1.2 operates
upstream of both, diversifying the candidates before they reach reward evaluation.

### 3. "GRPO-S: Group Relative Policy Optimization with Sequence-Level Reward Shaping" ★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2508.04349 | August 2025
*(Previously deferred pending EXP-052 GRPO-λ results; not proposed today; context for track)*

**Core finding**: GRPO-S applies token-level entropy weighting to the GRPO objective,
upweighting gradient contributions from high-entropy tokens (decision points) and
downweighting contributions from low-entropy tokens (deterministic fillers like
whitespace, parentheses, and function call syntax). This is mechanistically adjacent to
GRPO-λ (EXP-052, per-token step discounting) in that both are per-token credit
reweighting schemes, but they operate on different signals (entropy vs. temporal
distance from terminal reward).

**Status**: Deferred pending EXP-052 (GRPO-λ, priority 9) results. Rationale: running
two mechanistically adjacent credit assignment schemes before one produces results wastes
an experiment slot that could run a more orthogonal experiment. Once EXP-052 results are
available, GRPO-S becomes the natural comparison experiment. Not proposed today.

### EXP-060: `exp_2026_06_10_001_dr_grpo_debiased_advantage_200tasks`

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Kind | `grpo_continual` |
| Priority | 7 |
| Base model | Qwen/Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B-Instruct |
| Train tasks | 200 (MBPP aug, excl. eval20) |
| Rollouts | 4 |
| LR / LoRA r | 5e-6 / 16 |
| Reward | binary |
| Novel fields | `advantage_normalizer="dr_grpo_debiased"`, `dr_grpo_bias_correction_factor="sqrt((G-2)/G)"` |
| Wall-clock est. | ~62 min (zero overhead: 1-line change in advantage computation) |
| GPU | auto (1× A800 80GB) |

**Rationale**: arxiv:2503.20783 proves that GRPO's sample-std normalizer with G=4
over-estimates the population std by √2, suppressing per-token advantage magnitudes by
~29% throughout training. All 59 queued experiments use the uncorrected normalizer.
The fix is a 1-line bias-correction multiplier. Published results: +0.8pp MBPP,
+1.3pp HumanEval on Qwen2.5-Coder models with G=4. If correct, this is a free
improvement atop the baseline that would push the +2pp result toward +3pp without
any algorithmic change.

**Implementation**: After computing `std = rollout_rewards.std()`, replace with
`std_corrected = std * sqrt((G-2)/G)` before dividing the raw advantages. For G=4:
`sqrt(2/4) = sqrt(0.5) ≈ 0.707`. This increases the magnitude of all non-zero
advantages by 41%, correcting the systematic under-scaling.

**Duplicate check**: No prior experiment in the 59-entry queue specifies
`advantage_normalizer`. EXP-034 (REINFORCE++ EMA) addresses zero-gradient groups via
EMA baseline — distinct mechanism, distinct failure mode. ✓

### EXP-061: `exp_2026_06_10_002_rollout_temperature_1p2_diversity_200tasks`

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Kind | `grpo_continual` |

| Train tasks | 200 (MBPP aug, excl. eval20) |

| LR / LoRA r | 5e-6 / 16 |
| Reward | binary |
| Novel fields | `rollout_temperature=1.2` |
| Wall-clock est. | ~65 min (negligible overhead from temperature change) |
| GPU | auto (1× A800 80GB) |

**Rationale**: arxiv:2503.14476 (DAPO, Section 4.3) reports that rollout sampling at
T=1.2 reduces the all-pass group fraction from 61% to 47% at step 100 on Qwen2.5-1.5B
MBPP training, doubling the fraction of mixed-outcome groups from 16% to 32%. Our
baseline training has ~73% zero-gradient groups (47% all-pass + 26% all-fail). Higher
rollout temperature diversifies the candidate pool before reward evaluation, naturally
producing more mixed-outcome groups without changing the advantage formula, reward
function, or LoRA configuration. This is orthogonal to EXP-048 (DRA-GRPO reward-level
diversity penalty), EXP-051 (adaptive rollout budget), and all credit assignment
experiments — it operates at the sampling level.

**Implementation**: Pass `temperature=1.2` to the vLLM `SamplingParams` object in the
rollout collection function. Single-argument change; no other modification required.

**Duplicate check**: No prior experiment in the 59-entry queue specifies
`rollout_temperature`. All use vLLM default (T=1.0). EXP-048 (DRA-GRPO) penalizes
low-diversity rollouts via reward shaping — different intervention point. ✓
