## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. RAPER: Rollout-Level Advantage-Prioritized Experience Replay for GRPO ★★★
**ArXiv ID:** 2606.04560 | June 2026

**Core finding:** The "generate-and-discard" paradigm in GRPO wastes the most informative training signal: rollouts generated at earlier training steps, when a currently-collapsed prompt was still tractable (some rollouts passing, some failing), carry non-zero advantages that are discarded permanently. RAPER stores individual rollouts — not whole groups — in a per-prompt priority queue ordered by |advantage| at generation time. When a current-step group has zero within-group reward variance (all K rollouts have the same reward), RAPER fetches the stored top-K highest-|advantage| rollouts for that prompt (filtered by staleness: max_age = 2 training epochs), composes a mixed group (70% fresh + 30% replayed), and computes group-normalized advantages over the combined distribution. Policy gradient uses stored log-probs as π_old for replayed rollouts (appropriate since the behavior policy was the model at generation time). Evaluated on Qwen2.5 (1.5B, 7B) and Llama-3.1-8B: +2.1–4.3 absolute pp over vanilla GRPO on MATH-500 and HumanEval. Training stability improves (lower gradient variance, fewer collapsed-group steps).

**Relevance to us:** Our ACR=52.4% is the direct target. For the 43 collapsed groups at each training step, RAPER injects real historical rollouts that had non-zero advantages. Earlier in training — especially cycle 0, or the first 5-10 training steps of any cycle before the policy overfits to easy tasks — these prompts produced mixed outcomes. The buffer captures exactly those high-|advantage| moments. The mechanism is cleanly orthogonal to all existing queued fixes: AVSPO (EXP-094) generates synthetic rollouts via ε-greedy, while RAPER uses real rollouts with known rewards; sign advantage (EXP-108) changes the advantage formula rather than group composition; TACO (EXP-116) suppresses credit in winning rollouts of NON-collapsed groups (the opposite target). Implementation requires ~50 lines in `grpo_train_simple.py` with no external storage (in-memory deque per prompt). Target improvement: ACR 52.4% → ≤35%, Full arm 92.68% → ≥93.5%. Motivated **EXP-118**.

### 2. MDP-GRPO: Stabilized Group Relative Policy Optimization ★★★
**ArXiv ID:** 2606.06058 | June 2026

**Core finding:** Provides the first formal taxonomy of z-score group normalization pathologies in GRPO. Three pathologies: (1) Low-Variance Amplification — small non-zero within-group variance inflates advantage beyond information content; (2) Mean-Centering Blindness — uniform positive reward produces zero advantage (lost reinforcement opportunity); (3) Zero-Variance Collapse — our primary problem (ACR=52.4%). MDP-GRPO proposes: (a) **Multi-temperature sampling**: K/2 rollouts at T=0.7 (conservative, higher pass rate) + K/2 at T=1.2 (exploratory, more diverse outcomes) within each group, increasing within-group reward dispersion; (b) **Dual-anchor normalization**: for groups still collapsed after multi-temp sampling, substitute EMA cross-batch statistics (window = last 32 steps) for the within-group mean/std; (c) asymmetric KL penalty; (d) prospect-theoretic advantage shaping (not directly applicable to binary code rewards). Results: +1.8–3.1pp vs GRPO on multi-constraint instruction following and code generation tasks.

**Relevance to us:** MDP-GRPO attacks ACR=52.4% at two stages. First, multi-temperature sampling reduces the probability that all K=8 rollouts fail: for a task with p=0.15 pass rate, P(all-fail) = 0.85^8 ≈ 27% under uniform T=1.0; with mixed T=0.7/T=1.2, the conservative half has higher pass rate and the exploratory half has wider reward variance — combined, P(all-fail) is lower. Second, for groups that still collapse, dual-anchor normalization provides a meaningful gradient direction using the cross-batch EMA. Neither mechanism requires a replay buffer, making implementation (~30 lines) simpler than RAPER. The EMA cross-batch anchor is a genuinely novel advantage formulation not present in any prior queued experiment (AVSPO uses synthetic samples, sign advantage changes the formula algebraically, RLCSD uses contrastive distillation). Motivated **EXP-119**.

### 3. CoRe-Code: Collaborative Reinforcement Learning for Code Generation ★★ (Filed)
**ArXiv ID:** 2605.24812 | May 2026

**Core finding:** Two-agent system: Planner produces abstract strategy (analogous to a skill procedure), Coder generates executable code. Joint GRPO training with collaboration-aware reward: Planner's reward tied to downstream Coder pass@k, not just plan coherence. Achieves better specialization than single-agent GRPO while being more sample-efficient. The Planner-Coder split emerges from collaborative RL without architectural changes.

**Relevance to us:** The Planner-Coder split maps directly onto our `f"{procedure}\n\n---\n\n{problem}"` format — the Planner is the skill extractor and the Coder is the small model. Currently, skills are extracted by a separate process (Phase 2) and GRPO is applied to the Coder alone (Phase 3b). CoRe-Code motivates joint optimization: score the skill procedure by the GRPO pass@k it enables, and update it accordingly. This addresses the Skills gap (75.61% vs 96.34%) by tightening the Phase 2↔3b feedback loop. Filed for EXP-121 (skills gap arm); higher implementation complexity than today's RAPER/MDP-GRPO proposals.

**ID:** `exp_2026_07_16_002_mdp_grpo_multitemp_dualanchor_humaneval`
**Kind:** `grpo_continual` | **Priority:** 7 | **Estimated GPU:** 2.5h

**Motivation:** arxiv:2606.06058 (June 2026). Formalizes zero-variance collapse as a z-score pathology and proposes two fixes: (a) multi-temperature sampling (K/2@T=0.7, K/2@T=1.2) increases within-group reward dispersion → reduces P(all-fail) at sampling stage; (b) dual-anchor normalization with EMA cross-batch statistics (window=32) provides non-zero advantage gradient for groups still collapsed after multi-temp. **Key distinctions from existing experiments:**
- EXP-108 (sign advantage): algebraic formula change (A=2r−1 for all-fail) → MDP-GRPO changes SAMPLING to prevent collapse in the first place, plus EMA anchor for remaining cases
- EXP-118 (RAPER): replay buffer of historical rollouts → MDP-GRPO uses NO replay, changes within-group temperature diversity instead — orthogonal and composable
- EXP-094 (AVSPO): synthetic rollouts for collapsed groups → MDP-GRPO increases natural within-group diversity via temperature variation + uses cross-batch EMA for residual cases
- **EXP-119: first experiment applying multi-temperature group sampling AND EMA cross-batch normalization together; the only proposal that reduces ACR at both the sampling and normalization stages without a replay buffer**
