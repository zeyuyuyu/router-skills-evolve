## Top 3 Relevant Papers

### 1. "F-GRPO: Don't Let Your Policy Learn the Obvious and Forget the Rare" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2602.06717 | February 2026

**Core finding**: Standard GRPO with binary rewards concentrates gradient mass on
mixed-outcome groups (where neither all-pass nor all-fail occurs), but within those groups
treats all prompts equally regardless of difficulty. This causes the policy to
over-optimize easy problems — spending gradient steps on prompts the model already solves
~75% of the time — while under-learning rare-but-solvable hard problems. F-GRPO adds a
focal weighting coefficient `alpha_i = (1 - group_pass_rate)^gamma` (gamma=2.0 default)
that down-scales updates on high-pass-rate (easy) groups and amplifies updates on
low-pass-rate (hard) groups, where `group_pass_rate = mean(r_1...r_G)` for the G rollouts
of prompt i. On Qwen2.5-7B code generation: pass@1 holds stable, pass@256 improves from
64.1% → 70.3% (GRPO→F-GRPO), 69.3% → 72.5% (DAPO→F-DAPO), 73.2% → 76.8% (CISPO→F-CISPO).
The focal weighting adds O(1) computation per group — essentially free.

**Why actioned today (EXP-068)**: Our +2pp GRPO result (47→49/100) is stuck at the same
ceiling that F-GRPO addresses. EXP-060 (Dr. GRPO, arxiv:2503.20783) corrects bias in
advantage normalizer denominator. EXP-061 (T=1.2, arxiv:2503.14476) diversifies rollout
sampling. Neither addresses the *distribution of gradient mass across prompts* —  F-GRPO
is orthogonal to both. Three-mechanism combination (F-GRPO + Dr. GRPO + T=1.2) not
proposed yet; EXP-068 isolates the F-GRPO contribution first.

### 2. "Smaller Models are Natural Explorers for Policy-Level Diversity in GRPO" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2605.30789 | May 2026

**Core finding**: Smaller models within the same family (e.g., 0.5B vs 1.5B within
Qwen2.5-Coder) produce structurally more diverse rollouts because they have lower
base-distribution entropy that hasn't been sharpened by RLHF alignment. S2L-PO
(Small-to-Large Policy Optimization) generates GRPO rollouts using the small model
(frozen, no gradient updates) while applying policy gradient updates only to the large
model. Diversity is *policy-level* — different solution strategies — rather than
*token-level* (noise injected by T>1.0 sampling). Reports +8.8% on AIME24 using 1.7B
explorer → 8B policy, with reduced rollout compute (fewer rollouts needed due to higher
diversity fraction).

**Why actioned today (EXP-069)**: Our history shows 0.5B consistently degrades under
fine-tuning, but S2L-PO uses 0.5B *only* for frozen rollout generation — this avoids
the 0.5B training pathology entirely. The 400-task degradation open question (confirmed by
EXP-063 curriculum diagnosis) likely involves entropy collapse of the 1.5B model toward
a narrow solution distribution. S2L-PO provides a rollout-level diversity mechanism that
prevents this collapse without curriculum scheduling. Complements EXP-062/063
(curriculum) from a different angle.

### 3. "Router-R1: Teaching LLMs Multi-Round Routing and Aggregation via Reinforcement Learning" ★★★
**ArXiv ID**: 2506.09033 | June 2026 (NeurIPS'25)

**Core finding**: Formulates multi-LLM routing as a sequential decision process where the
router LLM interleaves "think" (deliberation) and "route" (model invocation) actions,
accumulating responses in context before producing the final answer. Trained with
rule-based reward: format reward + outcome reward + cost reward (penalizes over-routing to
large/expensive models). Demonstrates significant routing quality improvements over
single-round static routers on MMLU, GSM8K, and code generation benchmarks.

**Why not actioned today**: Directly relevant to our MERA routing track, but Router-R1
requires RL training of the router itself — a substantially different architecture from our
TF-IDF+LR router. EXP-066 (tau2 router regularization, CPU-only) must run first to
resolve the held-out overfitting issue (routing acc 80%→66%). If EXP-066 fixes
overfitting with L2 regularization alone, the current router is sound and Router-R1-style
RL routing becomes a paper extension. If EXP-066 fails (vocabulary mismatch root cause),
Router-R1's RL formulation with cost+outcome reward becomes the primary mitigation for
the tau2 routing track. **Deferred until EXP-066 result available.**
